# Copywriting Frameworks Reference

Complete breakdown of each framework with structure, when to use, and templates.

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences, cold traffic, short-form ads
**Awareness:** Problem-aware | **Sophistication:** Low-Medium

| Section | Purpose | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Problem** | Name the pain they already feel | "Tired of [specific pain]?" / "Still [struggling with X]?" |
| **Agitate** | Twist the knife — make them feel it deeper | "Every day you wait, [consequence]. And it's only getting worse because [reason]." |
| **Solution** | Present the fix + CTA | "That's why [offer]. [Mechanism]. [CTA]." |

**Example (life insurance, Meta ad):**
> Still putting off life insurance because you think it's too expensive?
>
> Here's the truth: every year you wait, premiums go up. And if something happens before you're covered, your family gets nothing. No payout. No safety net. Just bills.
>
> Get a free quote in 60 seconds — no obligation, no pushy agents. Just real numbers so you know where you stand.
>
> [Get My Free Quote]

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Best for:** Product-aware audiences, warm traffic, landing pages
**Awareness:** Product-aware | **Sophistication:** Medium-High

| Section | Purpose | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Attention** | Pattern interrupt, stop the scroll | Bold claim, shocking stat, or unexpected question |
| **Interest** | Build curiosity with relevant detail | "Here's what most people don't know about [topic]..." |
| **Desire** | Paint the outcome they want | "Imagine [dream scenario] without [pain point]." |
| **Action** | Clear, specific CTA | "[CTA verb] + [what they get] + [risk reversal]" |

---

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Best for:** Aspiration-led copy, warm audiences, email
**Awareness:** Solution-aware | **Sophistication:** Medium

| Section | Purpose | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Before** | Current painful reality | "Right now, you're [current state]." |
| **After** | Dream outcome they want | "Imagine [desired state]. [Specific detail]." |
| **Bridge** | Your offer connects them | "Here's how to get there: [offer + mechanism]." |

---

## FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits

**Best for:** Product-aware, comparison shoppers, landing page feature sections
**Awareness:** Product-aware | **Sophistication:** High

| Section | Purpose | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Feature** | What it is | "[Product/service] includes [feature]." |
| **Advantage** | Why that matters functionally | "Which means [functional advantage]." |
| **Benefit** | What it means for their life | "So you can [emotional/practical benefit]." |

---

## PASO — Problem, Agitate, Solution, Outcome

**Best for:** Extended PAS with a vision of the result. Landing pages, VSLs.
**Awareness:** Problem-aware | **Sophistication:** Low-Medium

Same as PAS, plus:

| Section | Purpose | Template |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Outcome** | Paint the after-state | "Within [timeframe], you'll [specific result]. No more [pain]. Just [desire]." |

---

## Story-Based / Narrative Hook

**Best for:** Unaware audiences, cold traffic, long-form, VSLs
**Awareness:** Unaware | **Sophistication:** Any

Structure:
1. **Hook** — Relatable character or situation ("Last Tuesday, a 43-year-old dad from Ohio...")
2. **Conflict** — The problem they faced (mirrors the reader's problem)
3. **Discovery** — How they found the solution (your mechanism)
4. **Resolution** — The result (with proof)
5. **Invitation** — The reader can have the same result (CTA)

---

## Curiosity / Quiz Funnel

**Best for:** Unaware audiences, cold traffic, lead gen
**Awareness:** Unaware | **Sophistication:** Any

Structure:
1. **Curiosity hook** — Question that creates an information gap
2. **Qualifier** — "If you're [demographic], this is for you"
3. **Micro-commitment** — Small easy action (quiz, calculator, assessment)
4. **Reveal** — Personalized result that leads to the offer
5. **CTA** — Next step based on their result

---

## Social Proof-Led

**Best for:** Most-aware audiences, retargeting, bottom of funnel
**Awareness:** Most aware | **Sophistication:** High

Structure:
1. **Proof headline** — Lead with the result ("2,847 families protected this month")
2. **Testimonial/case study** — Real story from a real customer
3. **Reinforcement** — Additional proof points (stats, credentials, logos)
4. **CTA** — Join them ("Get your free quote like [name] did")

---

## Direct Offer

**Best for:** Most-aware audiences, hot traffic, retargeting
**Awareness:** Most aware | **Sophistication:** Any

Structure:
1. **Offer headline** — State the deal clearly
2. **Key benefit** — One sentence on why it matters
3. **Risk reversal** — Remove friction
4. **CTA** — Act now
5. **Urgency** — Real reason to act today

---

## Urgency-Led

**Best for:** Hot audiences, deadline-driven campaigns, retargeting
**Awareness:** Product-aware to Most-aware | **Sophistication:** Any

Structure:
1. **Urgency statement** — Real deadline or scarcity (never fake)
2. **Reminder of value** — What they're about to miss
3. **Social proof** — Others already acted
4. **CTA** — Last chance framing

**Rules:**
- Urgency must be REAL. Never fabricate deadlines or scarcity.
- If no real urgency exists, don't use this framework.

---

## Framework Selection Cheat Sheet

| If the audience is... | And sophistication is... | Use... |
|----------------------|------------------------|--------|
| Unaware | Any | Story-based, Quiz funnel, Curiosity |
| Problem-aware | Low | PAS |
| Problem-aware | Medium | PASO |
| Solution-aware | Low-Med | BAB |
| Solution-aware | High | FAB, Mechanism-first |
| Product-aware | Medium | AIDA |
| Product-aware | High | Social proof-led, Comparison |
| Most aware | Any | Direct offer, Urgency-led |
| Any (cold traffic) | High (skeptical) | Story-based + heavy proof |
| Any (retargeting) | Any | Social proof-led, Urgency-led |
