# FTT Guest Pipeline Learnings

## What Gets Kept (Pattern from 2026-03-08 run)

The FTT audience (men 18-30) responds to guests who are:
- **Practical psychology experts who help men directly:** Joe Navarro (body language), Michael Gervais (performance psych for athletes). NOT academic psychologists like Jonathan Haidt or William Costello, who were later cut as too academic for the audience.
- **Authors with science-backed discomfort/resilience frameworks:** Michael Easter (The Comfort Crisis).
- **Health experts connected to physical performance:** Louisa Nicola (neurophysiology + athletics). Not pure longevity/biohacking researchers.
- **Compelling life stories with substance:** Ben Mallah (poverty to $250M), Alex Honnold (free solo), Paul Rosolie (Amazon explorer). Extreme stories that inspire action.

## Hard Rules

### NEVER suggest A-list celebrities
Lionel Messi, Sylvester Stallone, Pharrell Williams, Snoop Dogg, etc. These are completely unreachable for FTT. Do not waste slots on dream-tier names that will never happen. Every candidate must be realistically bookable for a growing podcast.

### Guest must speak fluent English
Yoel Romero was cut because he can't do a full English-language podcast. Always verify the candidate can carry a conversation in English before including them.

### Always verify profile URLs actually work
Jorge Masvidal and Rudy Mawer had broken Instagram links. Before writing to Airtable, verify the URL resolves. If unsure, leave the profile field empty rather than risk a dead link.

### The bar is higher than "famous person in the right city"
Location-based searches (e.g., "Miami entrepreneurs") produce lots of names, but most aren't interesting enough for FTT. The question isn't "are they in Miami?" but "would a 22-year-old guy actually want to watch this person for 90 minutes?" If the answer isn't an obvious yes, skip them.

## What Gets Cut

### AI/Tech Founders - Almost Always a Miss
Palmer Luckey, Alexandr Wang, Amjad Masad, Balaji Srinivasan, Bernard Marr. Mike's audience doesn't care about AI infrastructure, coding platforms, or crypto philosophy. The ai_tech category should be deprioritized or limited to 1 slot max with someone who has genuine crossover appeal (e.g., a tech founder who also talks about discipline/lifestyle).

### Too Corporate/Futurist
Peter Diamandis, Jesse Itzler. These skew older and more "business conference" than FTT's lane. The audience wants applicable wisdom, not visionary futurism.

### Too Small Following
Annie Jacobsen (37K). The 100K minimum is real for FTT. Below that, it's a hard sell unless the person has an extraordinary story.

### Random World Record Holders / Niche Athletes
Sebastian Steudtner (surfer). Unless there's a compelling story arc beyond "I did something extreme," pure athletes don't land. Alex Honnold worked because of the Oscar-winning documentary and the psychological depth of free soloing.

### Investigative Journalists
Whitney Webb. Too dark/conspiracy-niche for the main audience. The conspiracy_alternative category should lean toward people who ALSO have mainstream appeal (like Graham Hancock who's been on Rogan).
