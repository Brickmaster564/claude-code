# Jeremy Harbour (Deal Junky) Guest Qualification Criteria

## Show Profile

Deal Junky with Jeremy Harbour is a business and deal-making show. Jeremy is an M&A entrepreneur, author, and speaker who has personally bought and sold over 60 companies. The show sits at the intersection of high-level entrepreneurship and deal-making.

**Audience:** Entrepreneurs, business owners, and deal-makers. People who buy, build, and sell businesses. Skews older and more experienced than typical YouTube business content. International reach with strong UK presence.

**Host:** Jeremy Harbour

## Two Guest Lanes

The show serves two audiences from one stage. Every pipeline run should target a **70/30 split** in favour of Lane 1.

### Lane 1: High-Profile Operators & Entrepreneurs (70%)
Real founders, CEOs, and operators with proven track records. These are the "big name" guests that drive views and credibility.

**What makes a good Lane 1 guest:**
- Built, scaled, or exited a real business (not just talked about it)
- Can speak to specifics: revenue, deal structure, scaling challenges, mistakes
- Has a following that creates mutual benefit (audience crossover)
- Engaging personality, can hold a conversation with Jeremy's style

**Examples (for calibration):**
Raoul Plickat, Timothy Armoo, Chris Voss, Cam Zoub (Whop), Domenic Lacovone, Grant Cardone, Daniel Priestley, Reid Hoffman, Jim Kwik, Neil Patel, Dan Martell

### Lane 2: M&A & Deal Specialists (30%)
People in Jeremy's core world: acquisitions, exits, PE, search funds, deal structuring. Smaller followings but deeply credible in the niche.

**What makes a good Lane 2 guest:**
- Active in M&A, business brokerage, PE, or deal advisory
- Has real deal experience (not just theory)
- Can share actionable frameworks around buying, selling, or structuring deals
- Follower count matters less here, but still consider mutual benefit

**Examples (for calibration):**
Ben Kelly, John Mullins, Paul Giannamore

## Minimum Thresholds

- **Lane 1:** Follower count is a rough benchmark for mutual benefit, not a hard gate. Someone with a strong business story and 50K engaged followers beats a generic influencer with 500K. Use judgement. Link to strongest platform (Instagram, X, YouTube, or LinkedIn). LinkedIn IS relevant for Jeremy's audience unlike FTT.
- **Lane 2:** No follower threshold. Credibility in the M&A space is the qualification. Still link to their best profile (often LinkedIn).
- Prioritize UK, US, and Dubai-based guests

## Hard Exclusions
- Lifestyle influencers or "entrepreneur" gurus with no real business behind them
- Generic motivational speakers
- Anyone without substance behind their following
- Pure content creators with no operational business experience

## Diversity Requirement
Less structured than FTT. The main diversity axis is the 70/30 lane split. Within Lane 1, aim for variety across sectors: tech, e-commerce, finance, real estate, media, crypto, etc. Avoid loading up on one industry.
