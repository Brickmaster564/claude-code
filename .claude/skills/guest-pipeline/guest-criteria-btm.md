# Build to Matter Guest Qualification Criteria

## Show Profile

Build to Matter is an interview podcast about founders building technology with genuine real-world impact. The show focuses on actionable insights: what can the audience learn from this person and apply to their own work today? It avoids ego-driven storytelling, hustle culture, and polished origin stories. Instead, it leans into honesty, uncertainty, and the real decisions behind building something that matters.

**Audience:** Early-to-mid career builders, aspiring founders, engineers, designers, and operators who believe technology should solve meaningful problems. MENA-first but globally relevant.

**Host:** Ayax Bader, founder of Bellman Media. Content creator turned founder. Approaches conversations as a fellow builder and storyteller, not an industry authority.

## What Makes a Good Guest

### Must-haves
- Building something with genuine real-world impact (not just incremental efficiency gains or "disruption" for its own sake)
- Can speak to specifics: decisions made under uncertainty, trade-offs, mistakes, pivots, lessons learned
- Thoughtful and reflective about the building process, not just the outcomes
- Motivated by impact, not purely by exits or fundraise headlines
- Willing to be honest about what went wrong, not just the highlight reel
- Has practical insights the audience can take away and use

### The Dual Filter: Impact + Audience

Every guest must pass both filters:

1. **Impact filter:** Is this person building something that genuinely matters? Would their story teach the audience something useful about building with purpose?
2. **Audience filter:** Does this person have enough of a following to bring listeners to the show? We need cross-pollination. A brilliant founder with 200 LinkedIn connections won't grow the channel.

### Minimum Thresholds
- **Following matters.** The show is starting from zero audience. Every guest needs to bring some reach. The minimum bar is roughly 5K+ LinkedIn followers or equivalent presence on another platform (Twitter/X, YouTube, Instagram). Higher is better. The sweet spot is 10K-100K.
- **LinkedIn is the primary platform** for this audience. Most BTM guests will have LinkedIn as their strongest professional presence. Always check LinkedIn first.
- **Must have some form of active online presence.** No LinkedIn profile or completely inactive accounts = automatic cut. If they can't be found online, we can't reach them and they won't promote the episode.
- **Must be realistically bookable.** Not Elon Musk or Jeff Bezos. Think founders who are active on podcast circuits, who benefit from exposure, who would see value in being on an emerging impact-focused show.

### The Bookability Test
Before adding any candidate, ask: "Would this person realistically say yes to Build to Matter?"
- **Too big:** If they only do tier-1 shows (TED main stage, Rogan, Bloomberg), skip them.
- **Sweet spot:** 5K-500K followers, actively doing podcast appearances or building their media presence. They benefit from BTM's audience as much as BTM benefits from theirs.
- **MENA connection is a bonus:** Founders based in or connected to the UAE/MENA region are warmer leads because of the 24six9, Actual VC, and Tech Revolt networks.

## Guest Lanes

### Core Lanes

1. **Climate and sustainability tech** - Founders building technology that addresses environmental challenges: clean energy, water scarcity, carbon reduction, sustainable agriculture, circular economy. The MENA region has acute water and energy challenges, making this especially relevant.

2. **Healthtech** - Founders digitising healthcare access, building diagnostic tools, telemedicine platforms, or health infrastructure for underserved populations. Not wellness apps or biohacking. Real health infrastructure.

3. **Infrastructure and construction tech** - Founders building the digital layer for physical infrastructure: construction procurement, smart cities, logistics, housing. Particularly relevant given the MENA construction boom.

4. **Fintech for inclusion** - Founders building financial tools for underserved populations: unbanked workers, emerging market consumers, small businesses without access to traditional banking. Not another payment processor for rich countries.

5. **Education and dev tools** - Founders lowering the barrier to entry for learning or building. Developer tools, educational platforms, skill-building infrastructure. Directly aligned with Ahoy's mission.

6. **AI with real applications** - Founders using AI to solve specific, tangible problems (road safety, medical diagnosis, accessibility). Not "AI wrapper" startups or companies using AI as a marketing buzzword.

### Secondary Lanes

7. **Deep tech and frontier technology** - Founders working on hardware, biotech, or frontier tech with clear real-world applications. Atmospheric water generation, materials science, robotics for social good.

8. **Ecosystem builders** - People building the infrastructure for entrepreneurship itself: accelerators, communities, investment vehicles focused on impact. Relevant because of Ahoy's ecosystem play.

9. **Impact investors and VCs** - Investors who specifically back impact-driven companies and can speak to the landscape, patterns, and challenges of funding purpose-driven startups.

### Bonus
- Founders connected to the Actual VC portfolio or 24six9 community (warm introductions)
- Founders who've done a TED talk or similar high-visibility speaking (signals they can communicate well)
- Founders building in the MENA region who are underrepresented in global tech media
- Companies on impact-focused lists (B Corp certified, UN SDG aligned, recognised by WEF, etc.)

## Hard Exclusions
- Generic SaaS founders where the "impact" angle is a stretch
- Hustle culture personalities or motivational speaker types
- Self-proclaimed experts who've never actually built anything
- Course sellers or clout chasers with no real track record
- Anyone who just wants a platform to promote themselves without offering real insight
- "We raised a round" as the entire story, with no substance behind it
- Founders with no online presence (no LinkedIn, no social, can't be found)
- Unreachable celebrities or ultra-high-profile founders who only do tier-1 media
- Companies whose "impact" claim is purely marketing with no real substance behind it

## Calibration Notes

This is a new show with no past episodes to calibrate against. The first 3-5 guest batches will be a calibration exercise. We source, Ayax reviews, we learn what he keeps and what he cuts, and we sharpen from there.

Key questions to resolve through calibration:
- Is there a minimum bar on company traction (revenue, team size, funding)?
- How important is geographic proximity to Dubai for in-person vs. how open is Ayax to remote?
- Does Ayax prefer founders at the "figuring it out" stage or those with more proven traction?
- Which content pillars generate the most interest from him: Purpose & Impact, Reality of Building, Idea to Adoption, or Builders & Ecosystems?
