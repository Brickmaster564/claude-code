# Scale to Win Guest Qualification Criteria

## Show Profile

Scale to Win is a business leadership podcast about scaling companies. The show focuses on the practical realities of growing businesses: leadership, culture, hiring, fundraising, international expansion, and navigating the messy middle. The guest range is broad: from UK fintech unicorn founders to VCs who back scale-ups to niche operators building innovative platforms.

**Audience:** Founders, CEOs, investors, and operators involved in scaling businesses. UK-centric but internationally relevant.

**Host:** Dominic Munkhouse

## What Makes a Good Guest

### Must-haves
- Has real experience scaling, building, investing in, or advising growing businesses
- Can speak to specifics: growth metrics, scaling challenges, mistakes, strategic decisions
- **The person must be interesting, not just the company.** Running a profitable B2B SaaS is not enough. They need a compelling narrative: an unusual background, a dramatic scaling journey, a contrarian approach, a recognizable brand, or a story that makes you want to click play.
- A clear scaling story or scaling expertise that UK founders can learn from

### Minimum Thresholds
- **LinkedIn followers matter.** Guests need enough LinkedIn presence to promote the episode. Sub-5K followers is a red flag. The sweet spot is 10K+. A founder with 1,000 followers signals they're not active on the platform and won't drive listens.
- **LinkedIn is the primary profile platform.** Almost all STW guests have LinkedIn as their strongest (often only) professional profile. Always link to LinkedIn.
- **Must have an active LinkedIn profile with posts.** No LinkedIn posts = automatic disqualification. LinkedIn is the outreach channel. No presence = can't reach them, and it signals they're not relevant to the audience.
- **Must be physically UK-based.** Not just a UK-founded company. The founder/CEO must actually live and operate in the UK. If they've relocated to New York, Dubai, or Singapore, they're out for the automated pipeline. International guests can come through targeted/reactive searches only.
- **Must be an actual founder/CEO of a company with real scale.** 30+ employees is the practical minimum. Companies valued at 50M-2B are the sweet spot. Adjacent roles (restaurateurs, TV personalities, government officials, authors without a company) do not qualify no matter how impressive they seem.

### Best Guest Lanes (all UK-based for automated pipeline)
1. **UK scale-up founders/CEOs** - The core lane. Founders who've scaled real UK businesses. Fintech (Marshmallow, Plum, Token), SaaS (Paddle, Groove), healthtech (Cera), edtech (Multiverse), consumer (Little Moons, Childs Farm, TALA, Healf).
2. **Young UK exits and breakout stories** - Founders who built and sold companies. Timothy Armoo (sold Fanbytes at 27), Matthew Kelly (Neon Beach to £10M in 18 months). These are compelling, relatable stories.
3. **Interesting UK consumer brands** - The Little Moons, the Deliciously Ellas, the Trinny Londons. UK brands that scaled through creative strategies (viral moments, DTC, retail expansion). Not just tech.
4. **UK Top 1000 company founders/CEOs** - Sunday Times 100, Deloitte Fast 50, FT1000. Real companies with real headcount scaling. These are less "famous" but deeply credible operators.
5. **Niche operators and platform builders** - UK founders building innovative platforms in specific niches. Smaller but interesting scaling stories.
6. **Scale-up advisors** - CEOs who've scaled and now advise (Eric Partaker, Matt Lerner, Sir Richard Harpin)
7. **Corporate leaders with founder mindset** - CEOs of established UK companies driving transformation (M&S, Salesforce UK)

### Bonus
- Founders who've gone through an exit or acquisition and can reflect on the journey
- Founders who represent diversity in UK tech (gender, ethnicity, background)
- Companies on fast-growth lists (Sunday Times 100, Deloitte Fast 50, FT1000)
- UK-founded unicorns and near-unicorns

## Hard Exclusions
- Solopreneurs or freelancers (no team to scale, no scaling story)
- Generic business coaches or motivational speakers
- Social media influencers with no operational business
- Very early-stage founders (pre-product, pre-revenue) unless they have a prior exit
- Restaurateurs, TV personalities, or celebrities who aren't founder-CEOs of scalable businesses
- Anyone without an active LinkedIn profile (no profile or no posts = cut)
- Founders with sub-5K LinkedIn followers (not enough presence to promote the episode)
- Founders who are too famous to realistically book (e.g. Ben Francis / Gymshark level)
- Dated exits with no current activity: someone who sold their company 5-10+ years ago and isn't building or investing in anything now
- Small consumer/lifestyle brands without proven scale (sub-30 employees, no meaningful revenue). The bar: Huel, BrewDog, Lucky Saint = yes. Small wellness/fragrance/niche DTC brands = no.
- **Fashion/streetwear brands.** Not relevant to the scaling audience.
- **Founders not physically based in the UK.** UK-founded company with a founder living abroad = cut.
- **"Boring" B2B without a narrative hook.** A fintech or SaaS that's technically scaled but has no compelling story, no recognizable brand, and a founder with no personal profile = cut. The test: would a listener recognize the company or be intrigued by the founder's story? If neither, they're out.

### What survives (the DNA)
Across both rounds, the kept candidates share these traits:
1. **Recognizable brand or company** that listeners would know or be intrigued by (Gousto, Huel, BrewDog, Cleo, Synthesia, OVO Energy)
2. **Strong LinkedIn presence** with active posting and meaningful follower count (10K+)
3. **Compelling personal narrative**, not just "runs a company that scaled." Something that makes you want to hear their story.
4. **Currently in the game**, actively building or recently transitioned to something new
5. **Would actually drive listens.** The promotional value test: would this person sharing the episode on LinkedIn generate meaningful impressions?

## Diversity Requirement
Less category-driven than FTT. The main diversity axes are:
- **Lane variety:** Mix across the guest lanes. Don't load up on fintech unicorns. Include consumer brands, exits, Top 1000 operators, niche platforms, advisors.
- **Sector variety:** Tech, fintech, consumer food, healthtech, edtech, marketplaces, services. No fashion/streetwear/beauty.
- **Stage variety:** Mix of currently scaling, recently exited (Timothy Armoo), and scaled/established (Stuart Machin)
- **Story variety:** Some big names (unicorn founders), some under-the-radar operators (Top 1000 companies), some interesting consumer stories (Little Moons going viral). Every candidate must pass the "would I click play?" test.
- **Background variety:** Mix of genders, ethnicities, and non-traditional founder paths
- **Geography:** UK-based only for the automated pipeline (physically based in the UK, not just UK-founded)
