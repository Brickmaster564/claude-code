# FTT Guest Qualification Criteria

## Show Profile

First Things THRST is for ambitious men who want real transformation in wealth, performance, identity, and execution. Not surface motivation.

**Audience:** Men 18-30, UK-first then US and English-speaking markets. Heavy self-improvement consumers with low implementation consistency. Core tension: "I know what to do, but I'm not doing it consistently."

**Host:** Mike Thurston - fitness/lifestyle creator based in Dubai. His world is fitness, entrepreneurship, and personal development content. Guests should feel like a natural fit for a conversation with Mike.

## The FTT Guest Profile

The ideal FTT guest is **internet-native, socially present, and accessible**. They built their audience through content, social media, or direct-to-consumer businesses. They speak the language of Mike's audience (young men seeking transformation) and would realistically say yes to an emerging men's podcast.

**Think:** People in Mike's orbit. Fitness world figures, content creators, young entrepreneurs, authors who speak directly to young men, health/performance optimizers, provocative thinkers with social media presence.

**NOT:** Corporate executives, Silicon Valley leaders, AI researchers, ivory tower academics, A-list celebrities, or anyone whose primary world isn't online content and social media.

## What Makes a Good Guest

### Must-haves
- Credible operator with real outcomes, not just an influencer
- Strong on frameworks and practical execution
- Comfortable speaking with specifics, numbers, trade-offs, mistakes
- Substance-first, clear communicator, brand-safe
- Direct, high-energy, challenge-oriented tone
- Tactical and actionable, no fluffy advice
- Should help viewers "lock in" and execute fast
- Has an active social media presence on platforms FTT's audience uses

### Minimum Thresholds
- 100k+ followers is the rough benchmark, not a hard cutoff. The real test is: do they fit the show, have substance, and show genuine engagement? Someone with 80K and strong engagement who clearly fits the FTT lane is better than someone with 500K and no depth. There are also "undercover" high-value guests (e.g., Domenic Lacovone @iacovone) who Mike actively wants on the show despite smaller followings. Use judgement.
- Link to their strongest platform: Instagram, X/Twitter, YouTube, or TikTok. Not LinkedIn (wrong audience for FTT).
- Prioritize US, UK, Dubai flow

### The Accessibility Test
Before adding any candidate, ask: "Would this person realistically say yes to Mike Thurston's podcast?" Apply these filters:
- **Too big**: If they have 5M+ followers AND only do tier-1 shows (Rogan, Diary of a CEO, mainstream TV), skip them. People like David Goggins, Jordan Peterson, Andrew Huberman, Jay Shetty, Gary Vaynerchuk, Tony Robbins - these are aspirational but not realistic for FTT's current level. Exception: if they're actively doing mid-tier podcast circuits (e.g., Arnold actively does many podcasts, Patrick Bet-David is accessible), they can work.
- **Sweet spot**: 100K-3M followers, actively doing podcast appearances, YouTube collabs, or building their media presence. They benefit from FTT's audience as much as FTT benefits from them.
- **The "would Mike enjoy this conversation" test**: Mike is a fitness guy from Dubai. Would he naturally vibe with this person? A bodybuilder, a young entrepreneur, a provocative therapist - yes. An AI researcher, a political scientist, a generic health doctor - probably not.

## Guest Lanes

### Core Lanes
1. **Fitness/physique world** - Bodybuilders, fitness YouTubers, physique competitors, science-based fitness voices, fitness entrepreneurs, gym culture personalities. This is Mike's home turf and should always be well-represented. Examples from the show: Mike Israetel, Greg Doucette, Christian Guzman, Dorian Yates, Kevin Levrone, Larry Wheels, MattDoesFitness. Examples from the pipeline: Chris Bumstead, Bradley Martyn, Noel Deyzel, Alex Eubank, Samon Dauda.

2. **Young internet entrepreneurs** - Founders who built online, creator economy operators, e-commerce builders, agency owners, marketing entrepreneurs. People in their 20s-40s who built audiences and businesses through the internet. Examples from the show: Iman Gadzhi, Charlie Morgan, Jordan Welch, Luke Belmar, Matt Gray. Examples from the pipeline: Sahil Bloom, Alex Becker, Davie Fogarty.

3. **Wealth/business operators** - Entrepreneurs and business voices with real track records and a direct communication style. Not corporate boardroom types - think people who speak at marketing events, have YouTube channels, and communicate directly. Examples from the show: Alex Hormozi, Sabri Suby, Grant Cardone. Examples from the pipeline: Patrick Bet-David, Dean Graziosi, Ryan Serhant, Jordan Belfort.

4. **Performance and discipline voices** - Athletes, coaches, military-background operators, anyone who embodies discipline and can speak on it with energy. Must be accessible and media-comfortable. Examples from the show: Ross Edgley, Hardest Geezer, Devon Levesque. Examples from the pipeline: Jocko Willink, Bear Grylls.

5. **Psychology and identity experts** - Psychologists, therapists, relationship experts with practical, tactical frameworks. NOT academic psychologists or neuroscientists with research credentials. The test: do they give advice young men can use THIS WEEK? Think relationship dynamics, body language, confidence frameworks - not academic theory. Examples kept: Joe Navarro (body language), Michael Gervais (performance psych), Terry Real (relationship frameworks). Examples removed: Jonathan Haidt (too academic), William Costello (too academic), Vanessa Van Edwards (too niche). Examples from the show: Dr K, Sadia Khan, Peter Crone, Marisa Peer, Dr. Lalitaa. Examples from the pipeline: James Sexton, Jilian Turecki.

### Secondary Lanes
6. **Health/biohacking** - NOT generic health doctors or nutrition YouTubers. The health people who work on FTT have a provocative, unique, or extreme angle that resonates specifically with young men. Think: Bryan Johnson's "don't die" extremism, Gary Brecka's gene-testing biohacking, Dr. Paul Saladino's carnivore advocacy, Louisa Nicola's athlete brain performance work. Generic "eat well, sleep well, exercise" health content doesn't cut it - it needs an edge. Examples removed: Ben Greenfield, Dr. Eric Berg, Dr. Daniel Amen, Dr. Casey Means, Max Lugavere (all too generic/mainstream health). Examples from the show: Gary Brecka, Dave Asprey, Dr. Andy Galpin, Dr. Adeel Khan.

7. **Authors and thought leaders** - People who've written books that resonate with young men and have an active social/media presence. Not pure academics - they must communicate in a direct, relatable way and be active on social media or podcast circuits. Examples from the show: Robert Greene (in pipeline), Ryan Holiday, James Clear, Mark Manson. Examples from the pipeline: Jim Kwik, Robin Sharma.

8. **Conspiracy/alternative** - Geopolitics analysts, UFO researchers, alternative thinkers, provocative voices with media presence and entertainment value. Examples from the show: Dr. Steven Greer, Gregg Braden. Examples from the pipeline: Jeremy Corbell, Jimmy Corsetti, Luis Elizondo, Douglas Murray, Annie Jacobsen.

### Bonus Lanes
9. **Accessible celebrities with stories** - Famous people who are media-accessible, actively do podcasts, and have genuine transformation, fitness, or business stories. Not unreachable A-listers. Examples from the pipeline: Steve-o, Ethan Suplee, Arnold (who actively does podcast circuits), Nick Cannon, Rio Ferdinand.

10. **Dubai/Middle East connection** - Entrepreneurs and creators based in Dubai or the Middle East, given Mike's Dubai base. Examples from the show: Saygin Yalcin, Afnan Khalifa, MoVlogs, ArabUncut, Alex Chehade, Lord Aleem, Fadi El Khatib. Examples from the pipeline: Anas Bukhash.

11. **Wildcard** - Adventurers, unconventional stories, world record holders, ex-convicts turned entrepreneurs. Must have a compelling narrative and social media presence. Examples from the show: Michael Franzese, Hardest Geezer, Cory Richards. Examples from the pipeline: Paul Rosolie, Alex Honnold, Colin O'Brady.

## Hard Exclusions
- **Corporate tech CEOs, AI researchers, and Silicon Valley leaders** - e.g., Jensen Huang, Sam Altman, Andrej Karpathy, Yoshua Bengio, Geoffrey Hinton (literally declined by the team). Mike's audience doesn't connect with this world. The only tech-adjacent people who fit are those building in the creator/health/performance space with active social media followings (e.g., Will Ahmed/WHOOP).
- **A-list celebrities** who would never realistically appear on an emerging men's show
- **Comedians** - unless they have a massive podcast/content presence that overlaps with the FTT audience (e.g., Theo Von)
- **Food vloggers**
- **Generic lifestyle influencer profiles** with no substance
- **Beauty influencers**
- **Niche sport celebrities** whose fame is limited to their sport (e.g., surfers, extreme skiers, big wave riders) - unless they have a compelling business/transformation story clearly beyond the sport itself
- **Ivory tower academics** without social media presence - no matter how credible their work is
- **Anyone without substance behind their following**
- **Guests who are too high-profile** and would not realistically appear on an emerging men's show. Examples of "too big": David Goggins, Jordan Peterson, Andrew Huberman, Jay Shetty, Gary Vaynerchuk, Tony Robbins, Wim Hof, Peter Attia, Peter Diamandis, Kevin O'Leary, Ray Dalio. These people only do tier-1 shows. Aim for credible, accessible guests - 100K-3M following range is the sweet spot
- **Generic health doctors/nutritionists** - e.g., Ben Greenfield, Dr. Eric Berg, Dr. Daniel Amen, Dr. Casey Means, Max Lugavere. Generic "eat well" health content doesn't fit FTT. Only health people with a provocative, extreme, or unique angle that specifically appeals to young men
- **Academic psychologists and neuroscientists** - e.g., Jonathan Haidt, William Costello, Vanessa Van Edwards. FTT needs practical, tactical psychology experts, not researchers or academics. The test: "Would a 22-year-old lad watch this on YouTube?"
- **Marketing gurus who are too mainstream** - e.g., Russell Brunson, Tom Bilyeu. If they're already everywhere on the podcast circuit and their audience is other marketers/entrepreneurs (not young men), they don't add value to FTT specifically

## Calibration: Understanding the Show's DNA

Study the previous guest list, current pipeline, and rejection data below. The pattern is clear:
- **Heavy fitness/physique representation** - Mike is a fitness creator. This is his world and his audience's world. The pipeline is full of bodybuilders, physique competitors, and fitness YouTubers.
- **Internet-native personalities** - Almost every guest built their audience online, not through traditional media, corporate roles, or academic institutions.
- **Young, direct, high-energy communicators** - Not polished corporate speakers. People who can hold a natural conversation.
- **Dubai/Middle East thread** - Mike's base means this geography is naturally well-represented.
- **Provocative and controversial voices welcome** - Sneako, Fresh & Fit, Tristan Tate have all appeared. The show doesn't shy from edgy.
- **Practical over theoretical** - Even the psychologists (Dr K, Sadia Khan) communicate in practical, relatable terms, not academic frameworks.
- **No corporate/Silicon Valley** - The pipeline has zero successful entries from traditional tech. Geoffrey Hinton was declined. This is not the show's lane.

### Rejection Data (use this to calibrate)
From the March 2026 pipeline runs, 47 out of 60 candidates were rejected (78% rejection rate). The main failure modes:
1. **Too big/mainstream** (28%): People like David Goggins, Jordan Peterson, Gary Vaynerchuk, Tony Robbins who only do tier-1 shows
2. **Silicon Valley/AI/corporate** (13%): All AI researchers and tech CEOs rejected without exception
3. **Generic health doctors** (10%): Generic nutrition/health YouTubers without a provocative angle
4. **Academic psychology** (7%): Psychologists with research credentials but no practical, youth-facing content
5. **Niche sport celebrities** (5%): Sport-only fame with no business/transformation crossover

The 13 candidates that survived all shared: direct communication style, specific tactical expertise, accessible profile (would say yes to FTT), and clear relevance to young men seeking transformation.

## Diversity Requirement
Each run MUST produce a mix across categories. Do not load up on one type (e.g., all fitness guests). The show covers fitness, entrepreneurship, psychology, health, conspiracy, and wildcards. Aim for representation across at least 5 of the lane categories per run. Fitness/physique can have the highest representation (it's Mike's core world) but should not exceed 30% of any single run.
