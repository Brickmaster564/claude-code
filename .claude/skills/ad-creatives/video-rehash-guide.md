# Video Script Rehash Guide

Rewrite competitor video ad transcripts for Client Network verticals. Extract the winning DNA from any niche and rebuild it with vertical-specific messaging.

## Core Principle

Never copy words. Only model structure. The original script is a blueprint. You extract the psychology and rebuild the messaging for our ICP using our vertical's language, pain points, and offers.

## Step 1: Analyze the Original Script

Break the transcript into its persuasion stages:

1. **Hook** -- What grabs attention in the first 3 seconds? (Pattern interrupt, bold claim, relatable statement, emotional punch)
2. **Problem Agitation** -- How does it make the viewer feel the problem? (Fear, guilt, frustration, confusion)
3. **Mechanism/Discovery** -- What's the "aha" moment? (New information, reframe, surprising stat)
4. **Social Proof or Story** -- How does it build credibility? (Personal story, authority, testimonials, stats)
5. **Offer Stack/Solution** -- How is the product positioned? (As relief, as simple, as affordable, as urgent)
6. **Urgency/CTA** -- How does it drive action? (Scarcity, simplicity, low friction)

Flag specific persuasive devices: pattern interrupts, emotional hooks, story pivots, metaphors, stat anchoring, belief shifts.

## Step 2: Translate to Target Vertical

For each stage, rewrite using the target vertical's resources:

### Load These Resources First
- `resources/client-network/{vertical}/copywriting-bible.md` -- Messaging pillars, tone, key phrases
- `resources/client-network/{vertical}/voc.md` -- Voice of customer data, exact language the ICP uses
- `resources/client-network/{vertical}/ad-copy-swipe-file.md` -- Proven ad angles for this vertical
- `resources/general/copywriting-resources/awareness-stages-ad-copy-playbook.md` -- Stage-appropriate messaging
- `resources/general/copywriting-resources/sabri_suby_ad_insights.md` -- Direct response principles

### Translation Rules

**Replace all brand/product mentions** with our vertical's offer mechanics. Map competitor concepts to our equivalents:
- Their "formula/method/system" -> Our offer's core mechanism
- Their "results/outcomes" -> Our vertical's tangible benefits
- Their "proof elements" -> Our trust signals (coverage amounts, payout rates, Trustpilot, etc.)

**Anchor in ICP realities** using VOC data:
- Use the exact emotional language from VOC (guilt, fear, procrastination, relief)
- Reference the situations the ICP actually faces
- Mirror how real customers describe the problem and the solution

**Build belief shifts** appropriate to the vertical:
- What does the ICP currently believe that stops them from acting?
- What new belief does the script need to install?
- Weave these shifts naturally into the flow, not as lectures

**Use our offer's proof elements:**
- Specific numbers, coverage ranges, cost comparisons
- Process simplicity ("answer a few questions", "free quote in minutes")
- Trust signals (licensed experts, no obligation, verified carriers)

## Step 3: Apply Quality Standards

Reference `winning-scripts.md` for the quality bar. Every rewrite MUST:

- **Short, punchy sentences.** No filler. No waffle.
- **Lead with emotional reality** the ICP already feels.
- **Reframe product as relief**, not a sale.
- **Include concrete numbers** (amounts, costs, timeframes).
- **Simple CTA** (free tool, few questions, no obligation).
- **Story-driven or direct address.** Never corporate or educational.
- **Mass appeal, high ceiling.** Speaks to anyone in the ICP, not a narrow segment.
- **Accessible language.** No jargon. Reads like a real person talking.

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Over-emotional wandering before getting to the point
- Corporate/educational tone ("Studies show that...")
- Industry jargon the ICP doesn't use
- Weak, vague CTAs
- Copy that sounds like a pitch instead of a conversation
- Narrow appeal that only resonates with a small subset

## Step 4: Format the Output

- Script format: line breaks after every sentence or natural pause
- No section labels in the output (the flow should feel organic)
- Max length: 2-3 minutes read aloud
- Emotional logic must flow cleanly: Frustration -> Discovery -> Hope -> Clarity -> Action
- The script should read naturally as if spoken by a real person (UGC narrator or spokesperson)

## Step 5: Awareness Stage Calibration

If the user specifies an awareness stage, write for that stage. If not specified, default to Problem-Aware (the most common ad audience on paid social).

| Stage | Hook Style | Body Approach | CTA Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Pattern interrupt, curiosity | Educate the problem exists | Soft, educational |
| Problem-Aware | "You know that feeling..." | Agitate, then reveal solution | Bridge to the offer |
| Solution-Aware | "There's a smarter way..." | Differentiate our offer | Direct comparison |
| Product-Aware | Social proof, results | Overcome objections | Urgency, limited time |
| Most Aware | Reminder, new angle | Reinforce decision | Simple, immediate |

## Adapting Across Verticals

The DNA of a winning script transfers across any vertical. When rehashing a non-related niche ad (e.g., fitness, supplements, SaaS) for a CN vertical:

1. Identify the universal psychology (fear, desire, status, relief, belonging)
2. Map it to the equivalent emotion in our vertical
3. Replace the specifics but keep the structure and timing
4. Use our ICP's language from VOC, not generic marketing speak
