**Date:** March 24, 2026
**Context:** B2B outreach targeting med-spas and aesthetic medicine practices as potential PPC clients for Marco (PPCSauce).
**Sources:** Reddit (r/MedSpa, r/estheticians, r/plasticsurgery), Alignable spa/salon owner forums, AmSpa (American Med Spa Association) blogs and reports, MedSpaMarketing.com, med spa marketing agency blogs (ThinkDMG, Kovly Studio, Middle Way Marketing, Stranded, Diamond Accelerator), Quora med spa threads, BBB complaints, industry benchmark reports (Pennock, VortiHQ, Workee).

## 1. The Core Pain: Clicks Without Clients

The single most important thing to understand about this ICP: med spa owners are not marketers, and they have been conditioned to distrust marketing vendors because every agency they have worked with has sold them activity instead of outcomes.

They do not care about impressions. They do not care about CTR. They care about one thing: **is the phone ringing with patients who are ready to book premium treatments?**

> "We're running $2k/month in Facebook ads and still can't get people to book."
> -- Reddit, r/MedSpa

> "My agency keeps sending reports with traffic graphs, but I don't see new clients."
> -- Reddit, med spa owner

> "$4,000/month for 'brand awareness': can't pay rent with impressions."
> -- Reddit, med spa owner

> "I feel like I'm throwing money into a black hole and hoping patients come out."
> -- Med spa owner

> "The phone isn't ringing the way it should. Consultations aren't filling up."
> -- Kovly Studio, characterizing owner frustrations

The disconnect is consistent: owners are spending $3,000-5,000/month on ad management and another $2,000-10,000 on ad spend, and they cannot trace a single dollar of that investment back to a patient who walked through the door. **67% of med spa owners cannot identify which marketing channels drive bookings** (ThinkDMG). **85% are unable to link marketing expenditure to revenue.**

## 2. Verbatim Frustrations: Google Ads / PPC

> "I'm spending hours every night on marketing that doesn't work."
> -- Med spa owner

> "Most med spa owners waste money on ads without realizing it, often because they were never taught the right way to run ads in the first place."
> -- ManagedMedSpa

> "Without conversion tracking, your ad data is meaningless."
> -- Stranded (characterizing universal blind spot)

> "A single misconfigured campaign can burn through $5,000 in a week with nothing to show for it."
> -- BestPPC blog, referencing real med spa scenario

> "Set-and-forget campaigns fade fast. Weekly reviews reveal patterns, wasted spend, and new opportunities."
> -- Stranded

> "Many med spa owners see PPC management as an optional expense, a costly mistake that typically results in wasted ad spend that far exceeds any 'savings' from DIY management."
> -- ManagedMedSpa

**Recurring complaint patterns:**

- **Broad targeting burns budget:** Practices set campaigns to target entire states or regions instead of a tight radius around the clinic. Clicks from 50 miles away never convert. One common pattern: entire monthly budgets blown in hours because campaigns were not properly geo-targeted.
- **No negative keywords:** Ads trigger on irrelevant searches ("cheap Botox," "Botox side effects," "Botox training," "how to become an injector"), attracting researchers and competitors instead of patients.
- **Generic ad copy:** "Get glowing skin today!" does nothing to differentiate in a market with 50+ competing practices per metro. No credentials, no specialization, no urgency.
- **Homepage as landing page:** Traffic lands on cluttered homepages instead of dedicated treatment pages with clear CTAs and booking forms above the fold.
- **No call tracking:** Most med spa bookings happen by phone, not form submission. Without call tracking, the practice is blind to which campaigns actually generate revenue. This is the most common structural failure.
- **Google policy disapprovals:** Healthcare ad restrictions around before-and-after images, medical claims, and "unrealistic expectations" language cause constant campaign disruptions. Owners without PPC expertise do not know how to write compliant copy that still converts.

**The cost of doing it wrong:**
- A skilled PPC manager reduces CPC by 20-40% while improving lead quality (PatientGain)
- On a $3,000 monthly spend, that translates to $600-1,200 in recovered budget
- Lead acquisition costs across Google and Meta for med spas have "roughly doubled over the past two years" (Kovly Studio), making efficient management more critical than ever

## 3. Verbatim Frustrations: Agencies & Vendors

> "I feel like I'm paying for smoke and mirrors."
> -- Quora, med spa owner

> "You try a different platform, or hire a different agency, or throw more budget at the thing that isn't working."
> -- Kovly Studio, describing the agency-hopping cycle

> "Agencies promising quick results often overlook the need for a long-term, strategic approach."
> -- Middle Way Marketing (characterizing industry pattern)

> "Despite your efforts, the results aren't coming in as you'd hoped."
> -- Middle Way Marketing

**The Agency Carousel:**

The pattern is nearly identical across the industry. A med spa owner starts with DIY marketing. Realizes they need help. Hires an agency or freelancer. Gets lukewarm results. Fires them. Hires someone else. Gets similar results. Concludes that "marketing doesn't work for my practice." (ThinkDMG, confirmed across multiple sources.)

This cycle poisons the well for every subsequent vendor conversation. Med spa owners approach PPCSauce with active suspicion because they have been through this loop at least once, often two or three times.

**What owners report about bad agencies:**

- **Opacity:** Reports full of vanity metrics (impressions, reach, traffic) with zero connection to actual bookings or revenue. "The #1 complaint about agencies is lack of transparency." (Surefire Local, med spa agency frustration survey)
- **Industry ignorance:** Agency doesn't know the difference between microdermabrasion and dermaplaning, cannot write treatment-specific ad copy, and treats med spa accounts the same as any local business. Owners want "a professional team that understands what you do, the types of clients you serve, and medical terminology." (Multiple sources)
- **Contract lock-in:** Long-term contracts (6-12 months) with no performance milestones. Owners feel trapped paying for services that produce nothing.
- **Asset hostage:** Agencies own the Google Ads account, the website, the domain, or the analytics. When the relationship ends, the practice starts from zero.
- **Set-it-and-forget-it:** Campaign launched, then neglected. No weekly optimization, no bid adjustments, no creative testing, no seasonal budget shifts.
- **No CRO:** Agency stops at driving traffic. No landing page optimization, no booking flow improvements, no intake follow-up strategy. Clicks arrive but never convert because the destination experience is broken.

**Average wasted spend:** $3,200/month on underperforming campaigns (ThinkDMG estimate).

## 4. Lead Quality Frustrations (Med Spa Specific)

This is where med spa VOC diverges from most other PPC verticals. The lead quality problem is not just about volume vs. quality. It is specifically about the type of patient the marketing attracts.

### The Deal-Seeker / Price-Shopper Problem

> "The leads you do get feel like a revolving door of price-shoppers who ghost after the first inquiry."
> -- Kovly Studio

> "You're getting inquiries, but they're all asking about price first."
> -- Kovly Studio

> "Consultations are booking, but conversion rates are low because prospects are shopping around."
> -- Kovly Studio

> "Most people are looking for the deal of the day or one-hit wonders."
> -- Kimberlee Bowen, To Make Beautiful Spa (Alignable forum)

> "Patients who are searching for discounts are more likely to cause problems than patients who find your practice via more traditional means."
> -- AmSpa, Pros and Cons of Groupon for Med Spas

### The Groupon Wound

This is the deepest scar in med spa marketing. Nearly every practice has either used Groupon or knows someone who did, and the experience is universally negative.

> "Very few people who are directed to medical spas via Groupon become regulars: most are simply in it for the deals."
> -- AmSpa

> "The greater the discount, the less likely it is that the patient will return."
> -- AmSpa

> "The only thing Groupon does for you is get people in the door. They take as much money as you get."
> -- Woody Lee Hampton, Alignable forum

> "I was overworking my body...the most I would receive is $5 per client!"
> -- Tammi Zimmerman, Serenity Thyme Massage (Alignable forum)

> "You would have to double to quadruple the number of clients to break even."
> -- Cedar Park business owner (Alignable forum)

**The financial math that burns:**
- A $500 treatment discounted 50% on Groupon, minus Groupon's 50% cut = **$125 in the practice's pocket**
- That same treatment at full price to a Google Ads patient = $500, minus a $50-80 CPL = **$420-450 net**
- The Groupon patient almost never returns at full price. The Google Ads patient, if well-retained, is worth $7,800+ over three years.

**In one attorney's experience cited by AmSpa, five laser burn lawsuits involved Groupon patients.** Discount-seeking patients are described as more likely to create complications, file complaints, and leave negative reviews.

### The No-Show / Ghost Problem

> "17-22% of appointments are no-shows, especially for new clients."
> -- Workee 2025 Med Spa Booking Statistics

> "Missed appointments cost clinics $1,200-2,000 per month on average."
> -- Workee

> "Up to 40% of new client leads abandon high-friction booking flows."
> -- Workee

Low-quality leads amplify the no-show problem. A patient who clicked an ad for "cheap Botox" and booked out of curiosity is far more likely to no-show than one who searched for "best Botox injector in [city]" and chose a specific practice. **Better ad targeting directly reduces no-show rates.**

### What "Bad Leads" Look Like in Med Spa

- People wanting a $50 basic facial who get pitched a $3,000 filler package
- Price comparison shoppers calling 10 practices to find the cheapest Botox
- Groupon/deal site refugees who only buy discounted services
- People researching for a friend or family member with no personal intent
- Competitors clicking ads to drain budget
- Patients looking for insurance-covered dermatology (eczema, acne prescriptions) who do not understand med spas are cash-pay
- "Consultation collectors" who book free consultations with no intention to proceed

## 5. What Success Looks Like (In Their Own Words)

> "A practice that attracts premium clients who commit to comprehensive treatment plans can see per-visit revenue of $1,000 to $2,000+."
> -- AmSpa (contrasted with $200-300 average for deal-seeker practices)

> "A single Botox patient comes back 3-4 times a year. A filler patient graduates to skincare, laser, and body contouring."
> -- Dean Garland

> "Smart marketing doesn't just acquire, it retains and upsells, with CAC mattering less when LTV is $3,000+."
> -- Dean Garland

> "Patients aren't searching for cheap Botox. They're looking for confidence in the provider's ability to deliver natural-looking results."
> -- Wellness Clinic Marketing

**What med spa owners actually want from a PPC partner:**

1. **Phone ringing with the right patients.** Not tire-kickers, not price-shoppers, not Groupon refugees. Patients who searched for a specific treatment, found the practice, and are ready to book at full price.
2. **Transparency into what the money is doing.** Real-time dashboards, call recordings, form submissions tracked to campaigns, revenue attribution. Not a PDF with traffic graphs.
3. **Understanding of their business.** Know the difference between Botox and Dysport. Know that CoolSculpting patients are different from microneedling patients. Write ad copy that sounds like it came from someone who understands aesthetics.
4. **No contract traps.** Month-to-month, performance-based. If results disappear, so should the obligation.
5. **Ownership of their assets.** They own the Google Ads account, the landing pages, the tracking. When the relationship ends, they keep everything.
6. **Seasonal awareness.** Summer campaigns for body contouring and laser hair removal. Holiday campaigns for gift cards and injectable touch-ups. Q1 "new year, new you" campaigns for weight loss and skin rejuvenation.
7. **Lead-to-booking support, not just lead generation.** Fixes to the booking flow, call follow-up recommendations, landing page optimization. The practice needs help converting leads, not just generating them.

## 7. Positioning Implications for Outreach

1. **Lead with the Groupon contrast.** Almost every med spa owner has lived through the Groupon trap or knows someone who has. Frame PPCSauce as the opposite: high-intent patients searching for premium treatments, not bargain hunters looking for the cheapest deal. "Your next patient is already searching for [specific treatment] in [their city]. Right now, your competitor is capturing that search. We make sure you do."

2. **Transparency is the #1 trust builder.** The single biggest complaint across all sources is opacity. Mention account ownership, real-time reporting, call tracking, and revenue attribution in the first outreach. These are not features. They are proof that PPCSauce is not like the last agency.

3. **Speak their clinical language.** Using actual treatment names (Morpheus8, Juvederm, CoolSculpting) instead of generic terms ("cosmetic procedures") signals that this is not a generic agency pitching a templated service. It tells the owner: "This person understands my business."

4. **The LTV argument closes the ROI objection.** Med spa owners think in terms of "cost per lead," which makes PPC look expensive. Reframe around LTV: "A $80 CPL that brings in a Botox patient worth $9,000 over five years is not an expense. It's a 112x return." This math is the most powerful thing in the entire pitch.

5. **Month-to-month removes the biggest objection.** Long contracts are the primary association with bad agencies. Leading with "no contracts" in outreach directly counters the #1 fear before it surfaces.

6. **Acknowledge the burn before pitching.** "If you've spent money on marketing that didn't deliver, you're not alone: most med spa owners have been through 2-3 agencies before finding one that actually works" is a more powerful opener than any feature claim. It validates their experience and positions PPCSauce as the informed exception.

7. **Position Google Ads as the anti-Groupon channel.** Google search captures patients with active intent: they are already looking for the treatment and choosing a provider. That is the opposite of Groupon, where the platform manufactures demand through discounts. Frame it: "Google Ads captures patients at the moment they decide they want Botox. Groupon captures patients at the moment they decide they want a deal."

8. **Call out the franchise threat.** Independent med spas are competing against Ideal Image, LaserAway, and SkinSpirit, all of which have professional PPC management and massive budgets. An independent practice running DIY Google Ads against those competitors is outgunned. PPCSauce levels the playing field.

9. **Service-specific outreach works best.** Instead of generic "we do PPC for med spas," reference a specific high-value treatment: "We help med spas fill their Botox calendars with patients who book at full price." Or: "We built a CoolSculpting campaign structure that generates $30K in monthly treatment revenue from $3K in ad spend." Specificity builds credibility.

10. **The phone call is the conversion.** Every other metric is upstream. Most med spa bookings happen via phone call. If the outreach mentions call tracking and recorded calls as part of the service, it signals an agency that understands how this business actually works, not one that stops at "generating leads" and calls it a day.
