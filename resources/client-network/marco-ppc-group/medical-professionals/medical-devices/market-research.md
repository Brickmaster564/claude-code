Target geographies: USA and Singapore. Two distinct sub-types: B2B device companies (selling to hospitals/clinics) and patient-facing/DTC device companies (selling direct to consumers).

## 1. Market Overview

### US Medical Device Market

- **$191.2 billion** US market size in 2025, projected to reach **$203 billion** in 2026 (Precedence Research)
- Global medical device market projected to reach **$680 billion** in 2026, expanding at **6% CAGR** through the decade (Marketing LTB)
- **7,711 enterprises** in the broader Medical Instrument & Supply Manufacturing category (IBISWorld, 2024). The narrower Medical Device Manufacturing classification counts **1,062 enterprises**, though this excludes many instrument and supply manufacturers
- Over **18,000 medical device manufacturers** and **25,000+ medical device facilities** worldwide, with a significant concentration in the US (FDA registration data)
- Market grew at **2.7% CAGR** between 2020 and 2025, with accelerating growth projected at **6.2% CAGR** from 2026 to 2035 (Precedence Research)
- The US represents **over 40% of the global medtech market**, making it the world's largest market (AdvaMed)
- **6,500+ medtech companies** operate in the US, mostly small and medium-sized enterprises. Over 80% have fewer than 50 employees (AdvaMed)
- The industry directly supports **519,000 US jobs** (AdvaMed)
- **Market concentration is extreme:** the top 4 firms account for nearly **76% of total industry revenue**, while the top 15 hold approximately 43.6% of market share. This leaves thousands of smaller companies in a highly fragmented long tail (IBISWorld, Market.us)
- **Company size distribution:** small enterprises (28% of market by count), mid-sized enterprises (42%), large multinationals (30%) (Grand View Research, 2024)

### B2B vs DTC Split

The market divides into two fundamentally different businesses with different marketing requirements:

**B2B (Hospital/Clinic Procurement):** The majority of the market by revenue. Includes surgical devices, diagnostic imaging equipment (MRI, CT, ultrasound), patient monitoring systems, lab equipment (centrifuges, PCR machines), implants (orthopedic joints, cardiac devices, dental implants), and minimally invasive surgical tools (endoscopes, robotic systems). Sales cycles are long, deal values are high, and procurement involves committees.

**Patient-Facing/DTC:** A fast-growing segment driven by OTC regulatory changes and e-commerce. Includes CPAP machines, hearing aids (especially OTC since 2022 FDA rule), orthotics and braces, home diagnostics (glucose monitors, blood pressure cuffs), TENS units, nebulizers, and wearable health tech. Lower price points, higher volume, consumer marketing dynamics.

## 2. Firm Economics: B2B Medical Devices

### Marketing Budget Benchmarks

- Top-performing medtech firms invest **5-10% of revenue** in marketing, with digital's share increasing year-over-year (McKinsey)
- Healthcare organizations have increased marketing budgets to roughly **7% of annual revenue** in 2026 (Promodo)
- Digital allocation within that budget has risen to **50-70%** for B2B manufacturers, reflecting the shift away from trade-show-dominated spending (Asymmetric Pro)
- Typical channel split: conferences 30-40%, digital marketing and SEO 25-35%, sales enablement 20-25%, brand and advertising 10-15% (Buzzbox Media)

### Key B2B PPC Challenges

- **Remarketing is prohibited.** Google bans all remarketing in medical spaces per health personalization policies. No remarketing lists in Search, no Display/YouTube remarketing, no dynamic remarketing for Performance Max (Ten Thousand Foot View)
- **Creative automation is banned.** Must disable dynamic search ads and URL expansion for Performance Max. Cannot accept Google's auto-generated creative recommendations (Ten Thousand Foot View)
- **Audience bleed between B2B and consumer.** Without careful keyword qualifiers (device, equipment, for sale, supplier), practitioner-focused ads show to consumer queries, wasting 90%+ of spend on wrong audiences (Ten Thousand Foot View)
- **Healthcare advertiser certification** required for restricted categories, and approval is ultimately at Google's discretion (Clarity Quest)
- **Ad disapprovals are inconsistent.** Photos of clinicians administering procedures have been flagged as "adult content," while illustrations get approved more easily (Clarity Quest)

### Procurement Process

Hospital and health system procurement follows a structured, multi-stage process:

1. **Need identification** by clinical staff or department heads
2. **Specification development** by clinical engineering and medical staff
3. **Budget approval** through capital planning committees (often annual budget cycles)
4. **RFP/vendor evaluation** managed by procurement department
5. **Clinical evaluation/trial** by end-user physicians and nurses
6. **Value analysis committee review** assessing clinical, financial, and operational impact
7. **Contract negotiation** involving legal, compliance, and finance
8. **Final approval** by C-suite or board (for large capital purchases)

Capital equipment follows substantially more complex protocols than disposable supplies. Hospital systems employ more rigid evaluation than individual clinics or surgical centers. Group Purchasing Organizations (GPOs) aggregate purchasing power across multiple facilities, adding another layer.

### DTC Marketing Dynamics

- **Insurance complexity** is a major friction point. Patients searching for CPAP or hearing aids often cannot purchase until consulting their insurance company, creating long decision cycles and high drop-off rates between click and conversion
- **OTC hearing aid disruption** since the 2022 FDA rule has opened the market to DTC brands (Eargo, Lexie, Jabra), driving up keyword competition and CPCs
- **Price shoppers vs. high-intent buyers.** DTC medical device searches attract a mix of serious buyers and comparison shoppers with no purchase intent. Filtering by hearing-loss severity, payment type (insurance, subsidy, private pay), and purchase timeline is critical to lead quality (Dolead)
- **Subscription revenue model** for CPAP accessories (masks, filters, tubing) and CGM sensors creates high customer lifetime value despite moderate initial purchase prices
- **Third-party payer interference.** Big-box retailers (Costco hearing aids), insurance-directed channels, and online discount retailers create price pressure that independent DTC brands must overcome through PPC differentiation

## 4. ICP Profile: Sub-Type 1, B2B Device Companies

### Who They Are

- Medical device manufacturers and distributors selling to hospitals, surgical centers, clinics, and health systems
- Company size sweet spot for PPC outreach: **$5M-$500M revenue**, 20-500 employees. Large enough to have marketing budget, small enough that they lack sophisticated in-house PPC capability
- Categories: surgical instruments, diagnostic imaging, patient monitoring, orthopedic implants, cardiac devices, lab equipment, minimally invasive surgical tools, dental devices
- Many are single-product or narrow-portfolio companies that built the business on sales rep relationships and trade shows, now facing pressure to add digital channels

### Decision-Maker Profile

| Role | Title Variations | What They Care About |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing lead | VP Marketing, Marketing Director, Director of Digital Marketing, Head of Commercial | Pipeline generation, MQL volume, cost per qualified lead, brand awareness among HCPs |
| Commercial lead | VP Sales, Chief Commercial Officer, SVP Commercial | Sales-qualified leads reaching the right decision-makers at target accounts, pipeline velocity |
| Executive | CEO, President, COO (at smaller companies) | Revenue growth, CAC payback, competitive positioning |
| Product marketing | Product Marketing Manager, Clinical Marketing Manager | Awareness of specific device among target specialty surgeons/clinicians |

At companies below $50M revenue, the CEO or VP Sales is often the marketing decision-maker. Dedicated marketing leaders become more common above $50M.

### How They Currently Market

- **Sales reps** remain the dominant channel: field sales teams calling on hospitals, attending surgeries, building physician relationships
- **Trade shows and conferences** (MEDICA, RSNA, AAOS, HIMSS) consume a large share of marketing budget
- **Referral and KOL (Key Opinion Leader) relationships** drive adoption, especially for surgical devices
- **Digital marketing adoption is low.** Most medical device company websites remain static brochures converted from print materials (Pontus Rehn, LinkedIn). Approval processes designed for brochures (2+ weeks per asset) fundamentally cannot support real-time digital engagement
- **84% of MedTech companies** believe skill gaps have adversely impacted their business, with 40% reporting this impact worsening over the last five years (RFDM Solutions)
- In-house marketing managers typically have experience in only 1-3 marketing channels, creating gaps in PPC, SEO, and paid social (RFDM Solutions)

### Why They Need a PPC Agency

- Transitioning from trade-show-dependent marketing to digital-first, but lacking internal capability
- Sales cycles are long and complex, requiring sophisticated multi-touch attribution that generalist marketers cannot build
- Google's healthcare ad restrictions (remarketing bans, certification requirements, inconsistent approvals) demand specialized knowledge
- Talented marketing hires stay only 1-2 years before leaving for higher-paying roles, creating perpetual recruitment cycles (RFDM Solutions)
- Expensive marketing tools (HubSpot, Marketo, Pardot) remain underutilized because nobody on staff knows how to operate them

## 5. ICP Profile: Sub-Type 2, Patient-Facing / DTC Device Companies

### Who They Are

- Companies selling medical devices directly to consumers through e-commerce, retail, or direct response channels
- Includes: CPAP suppliers and DME companies, hearing aid brands (especially OTC entrants), orthotics and brace manufacturers, home diagnostic device makers, wearable health tech companies
- Company size sweet spot for PPC outreach: **$2M-$100M revenue**, 10-200 employees
- Many are e-commerce businesses or hybrid models (online + clinic referral network)
- The OTC hearing aid market has created a wave of new DTC entrants since 2022, many of which are venture-backed and spending aggressively on customer acquisition

### Decision-Maker Profile

| Role | Title Variations | What They Care About |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing/Growth lead | VP Marketing, Head of Growth, Director of E-Commerce, CMO | ROAS, CAC, customer LTV, conversion rate, channel efficiency |
| Founder/CEO | CEO, Founder, Co-Founder | Revenue growth, unit economics, market share |
| Digital marketing manager | PPC Manager, Digital Marketing Manager, Performance Marketing Manager | Campaign-level metrics: CPC, CVR, CPA, budget pacing |

DTC device companies are generally more marketing-savvy than their B2B counterparts, but PPC complexity in healthcare (compliance, restricted targeting, insurance friction) often exceeds what a small in-house team can manage.

### How They Currently Market

- **Google Ads (Search + Shopping)** is typically the primary paid acquisition channel
- **Meta ads** for awareness and retargeting (where allowed, noting healthcare restrictions)
- **SEO and content marketing** for condition-specific searches ("best CPAP machine for side sleepers," "OTC hearing aids vs prescription")
- **Amazon** for products eligible for marketplace distribution
- **Referral partnerships** with clinicians, audiologists, sleep specialists
- **Email marketing and subscription programs** for recurring revenue (CPAP supplies, CGM sensors)

### Why They Need a PPC Agency

- Healthcare ad compliance is complex: Google's sensitive category restrictions, FDA advertising rules, platform certification requirements
- CPCs in categories like hearing aids ($8+) and orthotics ($8.76+) are among the highest in healthcare, requiring expert bid management
- Insurance complexity and long patient decision cycles mean conversion tracking and attribution need specialized setup
- Competing against well-funded venture-backed DTC brands (Eargo raised $300M+) and retail giants (Costco) requires sophisticated campaign strategy
- Small marketing teams (1-3 people) cannot stay current on Google Ads policy changes, healthcare-specific restrictions, and competitive dynamics simultaneously

## 8. Singapore Context

### Medical Device Market

- Singapore medical devices market reached **$3.23 billion** in 2023, projected to reach **$7.11 billion** by 2033 at **8.2% CAGR** (Research and Markets)
- Foreign enterprises dominate **over 80%** of the market, with major players including Abbott, Alcon, Becton Dickinson, Medtronic, and Siemens Healthineers (EDB Singapore)
- Singapore hosts **400+ MedTech enterprises** (global and local) across Digital Health, Interventional Devices, Robot-Assisted Devices, Diagnostics, and Patient Monitoring (EDB Singapore)
- The Economic Development Board (EDB) actively promotes Singapore as a MedTech hub for Southeast Asia, creating a concentration of regional headquarters and manufacturing facilities

### Medical Tourism Connection

- Singapore attracts **SGD 2 billion+** in medical tourism revenue annually (Credence Research)
- **500,000-700,000 medical tourists** per year from India, China, Malaysia, Indonesia, Middle East, and EU
- Medical tourism market growing at **7.2% CAGR** through 2035
- **78% of Singaporean patients** conduct online research before selecting a healthcare provider (Hashmeta), indicating strong digital search behavior
- Medical tourism operators and international patient departments at Singapore hospitals are potential PPC clients for driving inbound patient traffic

### Singapore PPC Landscape

- Google now allows telemedicine ads in Singapore with LegitScript and HCSA certification
- Healthcare advertising subject to MOH (Ministry of Health) advertising guidelines, though these do not have the force of law for devices specifically
- MedTech companies in Singapore often serve as APAC regional headquarters, meaning a single client could control PPC budgets across multiple Southeast Asian markets
- Digital literacy is high, and B2B search behavior mirrors US patterns, making Google Ads a viable channel for device companies targeting hospital procurement teams across the region

### Opportunity Assessment for Singapore

The Singapore market is smaller by volume but presents a quality opportunity: higher average deal values (premium healthcare market), regional hub dynamics (one client can mean APAC-wide campaigns), medical tourism advertising (patient acquisition from neighboring countries), and a concentrated cluster of 400+ MedTech companies in a small geography that can be reached through targeted outreach.
