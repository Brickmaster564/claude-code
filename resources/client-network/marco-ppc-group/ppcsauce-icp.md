## About PPCSauce.com

PPCSauce is a specialist PPC agency focused on increasing both the volume and quality of inbound leads for high-value service businesses (law and med). Through tailored Google Ads campaigns, combined with proprietary Google Analytics and GTM tracking setups, PPCSauce ensures clients attract leads that have no trouble paying for the client's services.

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Core Service | Tailored Google Ads Campaign Management |
| Differentiator | Custom Google Analytics and GTM Tracking Setups, Strategy |
| Primary Outcome | Higher lead volume and improved lead qualification |
| Starting Retainer | $2,500/month |
| Target Geographies | United States, Singapore |
| Minimum Ad Spend | $10,000/month (client-side) |

## The Problem We Solve

PPCSauce is purpose-built for businesses that are generating leads through paid search, but struggling with two core issues:

- **Insufficient lead volume:** not enough qualified prospects entering the pipeline
- **Poor lead quality:** enquiries from prospects who cannot afford the service, do not meet case criteria, or are otherwise unqualified

Through precise audience targeting, intelligent bidding strategies, and granular conversion tracking, PPCSauce filters out unqualified traffic and directs budget toward the prospects most likely to convert into high-value clients.

## ICP #1: DUI and Criminal Defense Law Firms

*High-intent legal leads at scale, filtering out unaffordable cases before they reach intake.*

### Firmographic Profile

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Business Type | DUI Law Firm, Criminal Defense Practice |
| Firm Size | Solo attorney to mid-size firm (2-30 attorneys) |
| Geography | United States, all states |
| Monthly Ad Spend | $10,000+ on Google Ads |
| Retainer Budget | $2,500/month and above |
| Decision Maker | Managing Partner, Firm Owner, Practice Administrator |

### Pain Points and Triggers

**Primary:**
- High volume of leads who cannot afford retainer fees
- Calls from unqualified cases (e.g., minor violations with no defense)
- Intake team overwhelmed by tire-kickers
- Wasted ad spend on broad match traffic
- Difficulty tracking which campaigns produce retained clients (not just calls)

**Secondary:**
- Competitor firms dominating top search positions
- Seasonal fluctuations in case quality
- No clear conversion tracking beyond form fills or calls
- Over-reliance on a single lead channel
- Poor Google Ads account structure from a generalist agency

### What They Are Looking For

- A PPC partner who understands legal advertising compliance and nuance
- Campaigns that attract prospective clients who can afford a $3,000-$15,000+ retainer
- Tracking that connects ad spend directly to retained cases, not just calls
- Reduced intake burden through pre-qualified, high-intent traffic
- Transparent reporting and ROI accountability

### Outreach Angle

Lead with the qualification problem. The biggest frustration in legal PPC is not a lack of leads, it is the cost and time wasted on people who will never become clients. PPCSauce's tracking setup identifies and eliminates unqualified traffic at the campaign level, so the intake team spends time on real prospects.

## ICP #2: Family Law Practices

*Attracting high-value family law cases (divorce, custody, asset division) while screening out low-budget enquiries.*

### Firmographic Profile

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Business Type | Family Law Firm, Divorce and Custody Practice |
| Firm Size | Solo to mid-size (2-20 attorneys) |
| Geography | United States, all states |
| Monthly Ad Spend | $10,000+ on Google Ads |
| Retainer Budget | $2,500/month and above |
| Decision Maker | Managing Partner, Firm Owner, Operations Manager |

### Pain Points and Triggers

**Primary:**
- Attracting divorcing couples with significant assets vs. low-complexity cases
- High competition in metro markets driving up CPCs
- Leads who expect free consultations with no intent to retain
- No visibility into which keywords attract high-value cases
- Generalist agency with no family law vertical experience

**Secondary:**
- Seasonal peaks (Q1 post-holiday divorce season) with poor campaign preparation
- Difficulty scaling beyond referral-based growth
- Campaigns driving volume without case value context
- Underperforming landing pages with poor conversion rates
- No remarketing strategy for high-consideration prospects

### What They Are Looking For

- Campaigns optimised for high-asset divorce, complex custody, and business dissolution cases
- Lead qualification at the ad and landing page level (financial screening, case complexity)
- Tracking that connects campaigns to retained client revenue, not just consultation bookings
- A specialist agency that understands legal ethics, bar advertising rules, and family law buyer intent
- Consistent monthly lead flow, reducing dependency on referrals

### Outreach Angle

Family law firms are acutely aware of the difference between a $1,500 uncontested divorce and a $50,000 complex asset division case. Position PPCSauce as the agency that uses campaign structure, ad copy, and landing page design to self-select for the high-value cases and actively repel the low-margin ones.

## ICP #3: Private Medical Professionals and Medical Device Companies

*Connecting elective and high-value medical services with patients who can afford and are ready to commit.*

### Firmographic Profile

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Business Type | Private Medical Practice, Cosmetic Surgery Clinic, High-End Dental Practice, Medical Device Company |
| Org Size | Small private clinic to regional multi-location group; B2B device firms targeting procurement |
| Geography | United States, Singapore |
| Monthly Ad Spend | $10,000+ on Google Ads |
| Retainer Budget | $2,500/month and above |
| Decision Maker | Practice Owner, Clinic Director, Marketing Manager, VP Sales (device companies) |

### Sub-Segments

**Private Medical Professionals:**
- Cosmetic and plastic surgeons
- High-end dental practices (implants, veneers, orthodontics)
- Private dermatology clinics
- Aesthetic medicine and med-spa operators
- IVF and fertility clinics

**Medical Device Companies:**
- Medical device companies targeting hospital procurement
- Diagnostic equipment providers
- Minimally invasive surgical device brands
- Patient-facing device companies (CPAP, hearing aids, orthotics)
- Singapore-based medical tourism operators

### Pain Points and Triggers

**Primary:**
- Patients enquiring about procedures they cannot afford (e.g., cosmetic surgery leads with no financial intent)
- High CPCs in competitive metropolitan markets
- Calls and forms from people seeking bulk discounts or low-cost options
- No clear tracking of which campaigns produce booked consultations vs. enquiries
- Campaigns not differentiating between elective patients and insurance-dependent patients

**Secondary:**
- Poor lead quality from broad or generic medical keywords
- Seasonal demand gaps (e.g., post-summer cosmetic slowdown)
- Unable to scale patient acquisition beyond existing referral base
- Weak landing page conversion for high-consideration purchase decisions
- No experience with HIPAA-compliant tracking setups (USA)

### What They Are Looking For

- Google Ads campaigns that attract patients actively researching and financially prepared for elective procedures
- Tracking infrastructure that connects ad spend to booked consultations and completed procedures
- Compliance-aware campaign management (HIPAA considerations for US practices; PDPA for Singapore)
- Landing pages and ad copy that pre-qualify on price and intent, reducing low-value consultation slots
- A data-driven agency that understands long consideration cycles for high-cost medical decisions

### Outreach Angle

Medical professionals in elective and private-pay spaces lose significant revenue not from a lack of enquiries, but from consultations that never convert because the prospect was never financially qualified. PPCSauce builds campaigns that use price signals, intent-based keywords, and conversion tracking to ensure the enquiries that arrive are from patients ready to book.

## Disqualification Criteria

- Ad spend below $10,000/month: insufficient budget to generate meaningful data and results
- Unwillingness to implement tracking recommendations (GTM, GA4 custom events)
- Primarily insurance-dependent or NHS/public-funded medical practices (no control over patient intake pricing)
- Businesses based outside the USA or Singapore unless a strong exception case exists
- Businesses that have been burned by PPC and are unwilling to commit to a minimum 3-month engagement (unless there's a clear opportunity their last agency was poor and we can pinpoint why and how)

## Recommended Qualifying Questions for Sales Conversations

1. What is your current monthly Google Ads spend?
2. Are you currently working with a PPC agency, or managing ads in-house?
3. What does a high-quality lead look like for your business, and what does a bad one look like?
4. Do you have Google Analytics 4 and Google Tag Manager implemented on your website?
5. What is the average value of a new client or patient to your practice?
6. What is your current cost per lead, and are you satisfied with the quality of those leads?
7. Have you attempted to track lead quality or client value back to specific ad campaigns before?
