# Law — Divorce & Family Law: Market Research

**Date:** March 20, 2026
**Context:** B2B outreach targeting divorce/family law attorneys as potential PPC clients for Marco (PPCScout). Not a PPL vertical — no fulfillment, no lead economics needed. Research purpose: understand the ICP, find them, and write outreach that lands.

---

## 1. Market Overview

- **56,771** family law and divorce lawyer businesses operating in the US (IBISWorld, 2025)
- Market is highly fragmented: no single firm holds more than 5% market share
- **75%+ of all law firms** have fewer than 6 attorneys — solo and tiny firms dominate
- Solo practices = **40% of all law firms**
- Annual industry revenue: **$13.1 billion**
- ~3.8 million family law cases handled in state courts annually (custody, divorce, support, paternity)

## 3. ICP — Subgroup 1A: Personal / Family Law

**Who they are:**
- Solo attorneys or 2–5 attorney firms specializing in divorce, custody, property division, alimony, child support
- Typically owner-operated: the attorney IS the business owner, the marketer, and the decision-maker
- Located in US metros and mid-sized cities — highest competition and spend in LA, NYC, Chicago, Miami, Houston, Phoenix, Atlanta, Dallas

**What they sell:**
- Divorce representation (contested and uncontested)
- Child custody and modification
- Property division and high-asset divorce
- Alimony/spousal support
- Prenuptial agreements
- Post-divorce modifications

**Decision-maker profile:**
- Solo: the attorney themselves — one person controls budget, vendor selection, and results evaluation simultaneously
- Small firm (2–5 attorneys): managing partner or founding partner; office manager handles admin but not strategy
- 86% fail to collect email addresses during intake
- 42% take 3+ days to respond to online inquiries
- Only **14% of solo firms** maintain a formal marketing budget
- **26% don't track leads at all** (Clio)
- Highly unsophisticated buyers of digital marketing — burned often, trust eroded

**How they find vendors:**
- 43% Google search, 43% peer referrals from other attorneys
- 46% check Google reviews/Clutch before reaching out even when referred
- Bar association contacts carry significant credibility
- Most have tried a directory (Avvo, FindLaw, Martindale) and been burned

## 4. ICP — Subgroup 1B: Criminal Defense / DUI

**Who they are:**
- Solo attorneys and small firms (1–5) specializing in DUI defense, criminal defense, or a combination of criminal + family law
- Similar owner-operated structure; attorney = decision-maker

**What they sell:**
- DUI defense (highest urgency: client needs a lawyer within 24–48 hours of arrest)
- Criminal defense (assault, drug charges, white-collar)
- Expungement and record clearing

**Why they're valuable as PPC clients:**
- Time-sensitive searches make Google Ads disproportionately effective: "DUI lawyer near me" at 2 AM from someone who just got arrested is pure high-intent traffic
- Call-only campaigns and call extensions convert extremely well
- Case values are high: DUI defense runs $2,000–$10,000 in legal fees per case
- Solo practitioners completely lack PPC expertise and know it

## 5. Competitive Landscape (Agencies They've Dealt With)

**Major bad actors attorneys have been burned by:**

| Vendor | Primary Complaint |
|---|---|
| Scorpion | Long contracts, proprietary CMS lock-in, account data held hostage, disengagement after sale |
| FindLaw | Year-long contracts, zero ROI reported by majority of reviewers |
| Martindale-Avvo | Fraudulent inducement claims, year contracts, negligible results |
| Avvo | Paid tiers with zero leads, database manipulation to extract payment |

These vendors have poisoned the well. Attorneys approach every new vendor with active suspicion because they've been burned by at least one of these. This is both a challenge and an opportunity: anyone who offers transparency, month-to-month terms, and real accountability stands out immediately.

## 6. Apollo Search Parameters

**For prospecting this vertical:**

| Parameter | Value |

| Industry | Legal Services, Law Practice |
| Organization keywords | divorce, family law, matrimonial, custody, divorce attorney, family law attorney, criminal defense, DUI defense |
| Person titles | Attorney, Managing Partner, Owner, Founder, Principal, Partner, Solo Attorney, Divorce Attorney, Family Law Attorney |
| Company size | 1–10 employees |
| Location | United States |
| Seniority | owner, partner, c_suite, founder |
| Exclude | Legal Aid, Public Defender, Nonprofit, Pro Bono, Law School |

**Key exclusions (post-search filter):**
- Legal aid societies and nonprofit legal organizations (they don't buy PPC services)
- Public defender offices (government, not private practice)
- Law schools and clinics
- Large firms (10+ attorneys) — they have in-house marketing or enterprise agency relationships
