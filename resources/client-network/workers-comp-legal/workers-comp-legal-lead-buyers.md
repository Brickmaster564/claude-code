**Compiled:** April 12, 2026

This document catalogs companies that BUY workers compensation legal leads in the United States, organized by tier. These are potential customers for a workers comp legal lead generation business.

---

## Industry Context

The U.S. workers compensation system processes approximately **4.9 million claims per year** filed by private and federal employees, with total employer costs exceeding **$100 billion annually** and claims payouts of roughly **$62 billion per year**. The workers compensation insurance market is valued at **$56.7 billion** in net written premium (2025).

**Attorney Landscape:** According to Lawyers.com, there are approximately **59,392 workers compensation lawyers** and **34,389 law firms** across 14,279 locations in the U.S. California alone has 8,097 WC lawyers, and Florida has 3,469. Roughly 12% of the 135,000+ personal injury attorneys in the U.S. focus exclusively on workers compensation, yielding an estimated **16,000 to 20,000 WC-focused practitioners**, with tens of thousands more handling WC cases alongside other PI work.

**Average Case Economics:**
- National average WC settlement: **$44,179** (National Safety Council, 2024)
- Attorney contingency fees: typically **10% to 20%** of benefits/settlement (state-regulated caps)
- Average attorney take per case: **$5,000 to $8,800** based on 15% average fee on $44K settlement
- Average revenue per signed case for a WC firm: **$5,000 to $15,000+** depending on complexity, state, and settlement size

**CPL Benchmarks:**
- Shared leads: **$20 to $50** (sold to 3 to 5 firms)
- Exclusive web form leads: **$100 to $300** (varies by geography)
- Live transfer leads: **$100 to $350**
- High-claim states (CA, NY, IL): **$175 to $400** per exclusive lead
- Lower-benefit states: **$100 to $175** per exclusive lead

**Lead Buying Behavior:** About 80% of WC leads are generated through paid search (Google, Bing, Yahoo), with the remainder from Facebook ads and retargeting display ads. Law firms allocate 7 to 12% of gross revenue toward marketing. A typical small WC firm spending $5,000 to $12,500/month on leads would purchase 50 to 100 exclusive leads per month. Large firms and lead aggregators purchase at substantially higher volumes.

**Claim Denial Rate:** Approximately 10% of WC claims are initially controverted/denied, and 75% of denials go un-appealed, which represents a significant untapped market for attorney representation. Workers who hire attorneys receive 30% to 47% more in benefits on average.

## TIER 3: High-Volume Solo Practitioners & Small Firms

This is the "long tail" of the workers compensation legal market, consisting of thousands of solo WC attorneys and small (2 to 5 attorney) firms across every state. While each individual firm has a smaller budget than Tier 1 or 2, their collective volume represents the largest total addressable market for a lead generation business.

### Notable Examples

- **Earley Law Group** (Boston, MA): Led by Super Lawyer Christopher Earley, 600+ WC clients helped
- **Kajy Law Firm** (multiple locations): 2,500+ cases won, $40M+ recovered, 133 years combined experience
- **The Perecman Firm** (New York, NY): $500M+ recovered, 40+ years experience, NYC five-borough coverage
- **Rosenfeld Injury Lawyers** (Chicago, IL): Top-rated WC firm in Illinois, thousands of injured employees represented
- **H. Rosen Law, P.C.** (Pennsylvania): Dedicated WC practice
- **Workers Compensation Advocates** (Pennsylvania): Experienced PA WC practitioners
- **Loney Law** (Des Moines, IA): Iowa WC specialist handling denied claims appeals
- **Henness & Haight** (Las Vegas, NV): Nevada WC attorneys

### Why This Tier Matters Most

1. **Sheer volume:** With 59,000+ WC lawyers and 34,000+ WC law firms in the U.S., the vast majority are solo practitioners or small firms that lack the internal marketing infrastructure to generate their own leads at scale.

2. **Dependency on external leads:** Small firms cannot afford dedicated marketing departments, SEO teams, or large PPC budgets. External lead purchasing is often their primary growth channel beyond referrals.

3. **Easier to onboard:** Solo practitioners and small firms make purchasing decisions quickly, typically controlled by a single decision-maker. There is no corporate procurement process or multi-month sales cycle.

4. **Higher margins for the lead seller:** Small firms accept standard market pricing ($125 to $250 per exclusive lead) without demanding bulk discounts. They purchase in predictable monthly volumes of 20 to 50 leads.

5. **Lower churn risk when leads convert:** A small firm that signs 5 to 10 cases per month from purchased leads at $5,000 to $15,000 revenue per case becomes highly dependent on the lead source and unlikely to churn.

6. **Geographic coverage:** Small firms exist in every state, including underserved markets where competition for leads is lower and CPL is cheaper to acquire.
