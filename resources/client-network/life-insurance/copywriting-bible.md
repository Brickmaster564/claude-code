## 11. Under-the-Fingernail Copy Blocks

**Block 1**

That sickening feeling in the pit of your stomach when you're lying in bed at night, staring at the ceiling, and you can't help but wonder: What would they do if I wasn't here tomorrow? You picture your partner, their face a mask of grief, trying to figure out how to tell the kids they have to move. You think about the mortgage, the bills, the life you've built… and the thought of it all crumbling is almost too much to bear.

**Block 2**

It feels irresponsible, doesn't it? Being the main earner, the one everyone relies on, and knowing you have this one loose end. This one vulnerability. You've done everything else right — you work hard, you provide, you're a good parent and partner. But this one thing, this nagging thought in the back of your mind, keeps you from feeling truly secure. It's the thought that a single moment, a distracted driver or a sudden illness, could unravel everything you've worked so hard to build.

**Block 3**

She paced the floor that day, wringing her hands, saying "what am I going to do?" over and over. Her husband of 20 years was gone. And in the midst of her all-consuming grief, she was hit with a second wave of panic: the money. Could she afford the house? How would she pay for college? Her grief was suddenly mixed with a terrifying, cold despair. No one should have to face that. No one.
