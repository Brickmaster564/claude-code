Age Range: 25–55 (with outliers from 22 to 65+ depending on family status)
Gender: Slight male skew (56% male vs 45% female ownership), but rising demand among women, especially single mothers
Location: United States (all regions; urban, suburban, and rural)
Monthly Revenue: $3,500–$12,500/month typical household income
Professional Backgrounds: Full-time employees, self-employed professionals, gig workers, small business owners, educators, tradespeople, healthcare staff, IT/tech workers
Typical Identities: - Parents (especially new or expecting) - Breadwinners or co-breadwinners - Single moms, working couples, homeowners - Financially responsible adults looking to protect loved ones - Digital-first consumers who prefer online shopping and self-education
🚩 Key Challenges & Pain Points:
Pain point 1: - Confused by policy options (term vs whole vs IUL) - Don’t know how much coverage they need - Insurance language feels overwhelming or inaccessible
Pain point 2: - Fear of leaving loved ones burdened with debt or expenses - Lack of trust in insurers or fear that the policy won’t pay out - Worry that they’ll be pressured into a product that’s not right for them
Pain point 3: - Overestimate cost and assume they can’t afford coverage - Procrastination from emotional discomfort (death, paperwork) - Don’t want to deal with sales agents or medical exams
🌟 Goals & Aspirations:
Short-Term Goals: - Secure affordable coverage quickly and easily - Protect mortgage and children’s financial future - Check “life insurance” off the to-do list with confidence
Long-Term Aspirations: - Ensure family maintains lifestyle and stays in the home - Leave a financial legacy for children (college, inheritance) - Achieve peace of mind and feel like a responsible provider
🧠 Emotional Drivers & Psychological Insights: - Motivated by love, protection, and family security - Guilt or fear around what would happen if they died unprepared - Seek peace of mind and relief from the mental load of “what if”
