# 1. The Tax Relief & Debt Resolution Industry

The tax relief and debt resolution industry is a specialized sector of
financial services focused on assisting individuals and businesses with
significant outstanding tax liabilities with the IRS or state tax
authorities.

Core functions include: - Representing taxpayers before tax agencies -
Negotiating settlements or payment plans - Stopping collection actions
(wage garnishments, bank levies) - Navigating legal and administrative
tax processes - Bringing taxpayers back into compliance

Unlike general debt relief (credit cards, medical bills), tax relief
operates within a regulated federal legal framework.

Professionals typically include: - Enrolled Agents (EAs) - Certified
Public Accountants (CPAs) - Tax Attorneys

# 3. Types of Tax Debt Services

## Offer in Compromise (OIC)

Settlement with the IRS for less than the full amount owed.\
Approval rate in FY 2024: \~21%

## Installment Agreement (IA)

Monthly payment plan (up to 72 months).

## Penalty Abatement

Request to remove penalties due to reasonable cause or first-time
relief.

## Currently Not Collectible (CNC)

Temporary hardship status; collections pause but interest continues.

## Innocent Spouse Relief

Relief from liability due to unawareness of return errors.

## Tax Lien/Levy Resolution

Actions to withdraw liens or release levies.

## Wage Garnishment Release

Stops IRS wage seizure from employer.

## Unfiled Tax Returns

Assistance filing back returns to regain compliance.

# 4. Key Terminology

## Enrolled Agent (EA)

IRS-licensed tax professional with unlimited representation rights.

## Tax Attorney

Lawyer specializing in tax law and tax court matters.

## CPA (Certified Public Accountant)

Licensed accountant; some specialize in tax resolution.

## IRS Collections

IRS division responsible for collecting delinquent taxes.

## CP Notices

Escalating IRS balance-due letters; CP504 is final notice before levy.

## Tax Lien

Legal claim against property due to unpaid tax debt.

## Tax Levy

Legal seizure of wages, bank accounts, or assets.

# 6. Regulatory Landscape

The industry is heavily regulated due to past deceptive practices.

## Key Regulatory Bodies

### Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

Enforces against deceptive advertising (e.g., "pennies on the dollar"
guarantees).

### State Attorneys General

Investigate and prosecute tax relief scams.

### IRS Circular 230

Professional conduct rules governing practice before the IRS.

## Advertising Restrictions

-   No guaranteed outcomes
-   No false IRS affiliation claims
-   No misleading settlement promises

Messaging must remain truthful and compliant.
