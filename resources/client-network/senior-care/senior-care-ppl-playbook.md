## 1. Introduction to PPL Economics in Senior Care

The Pay-Per-Lead model in the senior care vertical is built on an enormous downstream client value. A single home care client generates **$27,000 to $60,000+** in gross revenue over their average tenure of 18-24 months, billing $2,400-$4,300/month depending on hours. An assisted living resident generates **$130,000 to $157,000** in lifetime revenue across a 22-month average stay at $5,900-$6,700/month.

This massive lifetime value supports premium lead pricing. Home care leads sell for **$25-$100 exclusive**, while assisted living leads command **$100-$400+ exclusive** or referral fees of **50-120% of first month's rent** ($2,000-$7,000+ per placement). The dominant model in assisted living is pay-per-move-in, with A Place for Mom charging communities the equivalent of one full month's rent per successful referral.

The critical metric remains **Cost Per Deal**, not Cost Per Lead. A $50 Caring.com lead that closes at 10% costs $500 per client against $60,000+ in lifetime revenue. A $20 Facebook lead that closes at 2% costs $1,000 per client. Both are highly profitable for the buyer, but the margin difference is significant for lead generation efficiency.

The senior care market has a structural tailwind no other vertical can match: 11,400 Americans turn 65 every single day, and 70% of them will need long-term care. Demand is accelerating and supply of qualified caregivers is not keeping pace. This creates sustained, growing demand for leads with no end in sight.

This playbook breaks down the practical economics and strategies required to build a profitable PPL operation in the senior care space.

## 2. CPL & Cost-Per-Deal by Platform

Different platforms attract families at different stages of the decision-making process. The adult child searching Google after their parent's fall is a fundamentally different lead than the one scrolling Facebook who hasn't yet realized Mom needs help. Understanding this intent spectrum is critical for building margin.

### Home Care Leads

| Platform | Avg. Cost Per Lead (CPL) | Avg. Close Rate | Avg. Cost Per Deal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Search | $20-$60 | 10-20% | $200-$600 |
| Bing Search | $15-$40 | 12-22% | $100-$330 |
| Facebook Ads | $15-$30 | 2-5% | $400-$1,500 |
| YouTube / Demand Gen | $15-$50 | 3-6% | $350-$1,200 |

### Assisted Living / Memory Care Leads

| Platform | Avg. Cost Per Lead (CPL) | Avg. Close Rate | Avg. Cost Per Deal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Search (Assisted Living) | $60-$150 | 5-15% | $700-$2,000 |
| Google Search (Memory Care) | $150-$400+ | 5-10% | $2,000-$5,000 |
| Facebook Ads (Assisted Living) | $20-$50 | 2-4% | $700-$2,500 |
| Bing Search | $40-$100 | 8-18% | $350-$1,000 |

*Sources: SeniorLivingSMART Google Ads Benchmarks [1], WordStream 2025 Benchmarks [2], WordStream Facebook 2025 [3], LocaliQ Healthcare Benchmarks [4], Focus Digital CPL Report [5], MegaDigital Bing Ads Cost 2026 [6]*

### Analysis

**Google Search** delivers the highest intent leads. Adult children searching "in-home care for elderly parent near me" or "assisted living cost" are actively seeking solutions, often after a trigger event (fall, hospitalization, cognitive decline). Senior care CPC has increased 32% YoY, signaling growing competition, but close rates justify the premium.

**Bing Search** is the most cost-effective starting point. CPCs are 30-33% cheaper than Google. Home services on Bing have the lowest CPA of any industry at $21.68. The demographic skews older and higher-income, which aligns perfectly with adult children making care decisions. Lower volume but superior unit economics.

**Facebook Ads** produce the cheapest leads but require different expectations. Most adult children on Facebook are not actively searching for care. They're scrolling past family photos when an ad about caregiver burnout stops them. The 2-5% close rate reflects this lower intent, but Facebook's conversion rate for senior living (9.21%) is actually the highest of any platform when measured click-to-lead. The key: Facebook generates volume that needs nurturing. Quiz funnels reduce CPL by up to 82% compared to traditional landing pages.

**YouTube / Demand Gen** is massively underused in senior care, accounting for less than 1% of typical paid budgets. Emotional video creative showing caregiver arrival scenes, family relief moments, and care quality builds urgency in a way text ads cannot. Estimated CPL ranges from $15-$50 but requires a longer optimization timeline.

**Critical Insight: Phone leads convert 10-15x more revenue than web form leads, and callers convert 30% faster.** Any funnel that drives inbound calls will outperform form-based funnels on a cost-per-deal basis. Click-to-call should be prominent on every mobile landing page (62% of senior care clicks come from mobile).

## 3. Lead Selling Prices & Unit Economics

The price a lead generation agency can charge depends on the lead's quality, exclusivity, care type, and delivery method. Senior care has two distinct pricing ecosystems: per-lead (primarily for home care) and per-placement (primarily for assisted living/memory care).

### Home Care Lead Prices

| Lead Type | Typical Selling Price | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Exclusive Data Lead** | $25-$100 | A real-time form submission sold to one buyer. Price varies by geography and care type. Memory care exclusive leads command the premium end. |
| **Shared Data Lead** | $15-$40 | A form submission sold to 2-4 buyers. Contact rates drop from 65% (exclusive) to 25% (shared). Speed-to-lead determines who converts. |
| **Live Transfer** | $30-$80 | A pre-qualified family member transferred live to the agency's intake team. Higher contact rate. Pay-per-call networks like Digital Market Media generate 50,000+ monthly calls. |
| **Aged Lead** | $0.25-$15 | Leads 30-90+ days old. Sold in bulk. Low conversion but profitable at volume with automated nurturing sequences. |

### Assisted Living / Memory Care Lead Prices

| Lead Type | Typical Selling Price | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Referral Fee (per move-in)** | $2,000-$12,000 | The dominant model. A Place for Mom charges 90-120% of first month's rent and care. Memory care placements can reach $12,000+. No risk to the provider: pay only on successful move-in. |
| **Exclusive Data Lead** | $100-$400+ | A real-time inquiry sold to one community. Memory care leads are the most expensive due to urgency and competition. |
| **Shared Lead** | $40-$100 | Sold to 2-4 communities. Lower conversion due to competition. ~2% close rate on aggregator shared leads (industry average). |

### Example Unit Economics (Home Care)

Modelling a scenario where a lead generation agency uses Facebook quiz funnels and sells exclusive data leads to home care franchisees:

- **Lead Generation Cost (CPL):** Agency acquires a lead via Facebook quiz funnel for $20.
- **Qualification & Delivery:** System validates the lead in real-time (confirms adult child decision-maker, parent's care needs, ZIP code in service area, timeline) and routes it exclusively to one franchisee via CRM integration.
- **Selling Price:** Agency sells the exclusive lead to a Visiting Angels or Home Instead franchisee for $60.
- **Gross Margin:** $60 (Sale Price) - $20 (CPL) = **$40 Gross Margin per lead (67%).**

In this scenario, the franchisee pays $60 for the lead. With a 15% close rate, their Cost Per Client is $400. If their average client generates $3,000/month for 20 months ($60,000 LTV), their Return on Lead Spend is **150x** ($60,000 / $400), which is exceptional. Meanwhile, the lead seller earns $40 gross margin per lead, scaling profitably at volume.

### Example Unit Economics (Assisted Living)

- **Lead Generation Cost (CPL):** Agency acquires a lead via Google Search for $80.
- **Selling Price (per-move-in model):** Agency charges the community $4,000 per move-in.
- **Close Rate:** 8% of leads result in a move-in.
- **Revenue per 100 leads:** 8 move-ins x $4,000 = $32,000
- **Cost per 100 leads:** 100 x $80 = $8,000
- **Gross Margin:** $32,000 - $8,000 = **$24,000 (75% gross margin)**
- **Community ROI:** Each move-in generates $130,000+ LTV at a $4,000 acquisition cost = **32:1 LTV-to-CAC ratio**

## 4. Lead Generation & Delivery Mechanics

### Funnel Structures That Work

**Quiz / Care Assessment Funnels (Highest Converting):** The clear winner for senior care lead gen. Quiz funnels produce a **277% conversion rate lift** over traditional landing pages and an **82% reduction in cost per lead**. The quiz format works because families are overwhelmed with options and genuinely don't know what type of care their parent needs. A quiz that helps them figure it out creates value before asking for contact information.

Structure:
1. Ad with emotional hook: "What type of care does your parent need? Take the 60-second assessment."
2. Quiz with 4-6 questions: who needs care, what help is needed, how soon, ZIP code, biggest concern, budget range.
3. Results page showing personalized care recommendation (Sabri Suby 17-Step System).
4. Contact gate: name, phone, email to "see your care options."
5. Thank you page with prominent phone CTA and scheduling option.
6. Immediate follow-up call within 5 minutes.

**Direct-to-Landing Page (Search Traffic):** For high-intent Google and Bing traffic, a clean landing page with a click-to-call number and a short 3-5 field form is most effective. No navigation links (no exits). Headline addresses the searcher's exact query. Trust signals prominently displayed. Optimized direct pages convert at 8-15% vs. 1-3% for generic forms.

**Content / Resource Funnels (Long Sales Cycle):** For families in the early research phase, educational content ("How to Know When Your Parent Needs Home Care") with a downloadable guide or checklist captures leads that need nurturing. Email sequences (5-7 emails) build trust over time. Best for independent living leads where the sales cycle is 120-315 days.

**Event-Based Funnels:** LeadingResponse averages 3,000 senior living seminars per month using hyper-targeted direct mail to 55+ consumers. Each seminar generates ~100 qualified prospects. Claims 4x higher conversion than digital. Offline model but proven for assisted living and memory care.

### Lead Delivery & Speed-to-Lead

Speed to contact is the single biggest conversion lever in senior care. **First responder advantage is massive.** The agency that calls within 5 minutes of lead submission dramatically outperforms everyone else. Best practices:

- Real-time API delivery to buyer's CRM (WelcomeHome, Aline/Sherpa, CareFunnels)
- Instant email + SMS notification on lead submission
- Auto-dialer trigger within 60 seconds for high-value leads
- Live transfer option for premium leads (bypasses speed-to-lead entirely)
- 25+ touchpoints are typical before a senior living conversion. Nurture sequences are non-negotiable.

## 5. Lead Qualification & Buyer Expectations

Home care agencies and senior living communities are increasingly sophisticated buyers. Lead quality complaints are the #1 issue in senior care PPL. Meeting buyer expectations on qualification is the difference between a sustainable business and chargebacks.

### Minimum Qualification Criteria

- **Decision-Maker Identification:** The lead must be an adult child or family member with authority to make care decisions. Not the senior themselves (in most cases). Women aged 45-65 represent 66% of decision-makers.
- **Valid Contact Information:** Phone number is the most valuable data point (phone leads convert 10-15x more). Email must be valid. Disconnected numbers or invalid emails trigger chargebacks.
- **Geographic Match:** ZIP code or city must fall within the buyer's service area. Home care agencies typically serve a 25-50 mile radius. Assisted living communities draw from a 50-100 mile radius.
- **Care Need Identified:** The lead should indicate what type of help is needed (companion care, personal care, memory care, assisted living placement). Vague "just looking" leads have dramatically lower value.
- **Timeline / Urgency:** "Immediate need" and "within 1-3 months" leads are premium. "Just researching" leads are lower value but still sellable at lower prices.
- **Ability to Pay:** Private pay families are the primary target. Medicare does not cover non-medical home care. Only 7% of adults 50+ have long-term care insurance. Medicaid leads are lower value (lower reimbursement rates).

### What a Typical Lead Form Collects

- Full Name (decision-maker)
- Phone Number (primary)
- Email Address
- Parent's ZIP Code / City
- Relationship to care recipient (son/daughter/spouse/other)
- Type of care needed (dropdown: companion care, personal care, memory care, assisted living, skilled nursing)
- How soon is care needed? (ASAP, 1-3 months, 3-6 months, just researching)
- What help does your parent need? (checkbox: meals, bathing, medication, mobility, companionship, driving)
- Biggest concern (dropdown: cost, trust, parent resistance, not knowing where to start)
- TCPA Consent Checkbox

### What Disqualifies a Lead

**For home care:**
- Outside service area (ZIP code filtering)
- No phone number / uncontactable
- Needs 24/7 skilled nursing (beyond non-medical home care scope)
- No ability to pay (no insurance, no private pay capacity, Medicaid-only in some markets)
- Already receiving adequate care
- Pure information-seeking with no actual need
- Duplicate / previously submitted

**For assisted living:**
- Severe medical conditions requiring 24/7 nursing care (beyond AL scope)
- Bedridden individuals
- Severe cognitive impairments causing aggressive or unsafe behavior
- Wound care requiring specialized medical equipment
- Communicable diseases (temporary disqualification)
- Care needs exceeding what the facility can provide

## 6. Seasonality in Senior Care Lead Gen

Senior care demand follows a distinct seasonal pattern tied to family behavior, health events, and the calendar. Understanding these patterns is critical for managing ad spend, adjusting CPL bids, and maximizing profitability.

**January - March (Post-Holiday Surge):** This is the #1 season for new inquiry volume. Families visit loved ones over the holidays and realize care is needed. The "holiday wake-up call" effect drives a significant spike in searches starting in January. These leads are high-intent. Adult children return from holiday visits determined to take action. Tax refund season also provides financial catalyst. **Scale spend aggressively in this window.**

**April - June (Spring Planning):** CPC traditionally peaks in April due to increased competition. Demand remains solid as families plan for summer logistics (who watches Mom while the kids are out of school?). Spring is also when families coordinate care transitions with real estate (selling the family home). This is a good period for content marketing and nurture campaigns. Moderate spend with focus on efficiency.

**July - August (Summer Planning & Moving Season):** Warmer weather makes physical moves easier (especially for assisted living transitions). Families coordinate with home sales. Occupancy tends to rise during summer months. Q3 leads may need longer nurture cycles. Solid demand but not peak.

**September - December (Fall Peak & Holiday Care Needs):** September has the highest search query volume of any month based on 3-year data. Fall is when families regroup after summer and accelerate decision-making before the holidays. November-December brings another wave as families gather and again observe their parent's decline. End-of-year moves driven by weather concerns (cold weather risks for seniors living alone). Q4 leads typically convert to clients in the following year. Flu season can negatively affect occupancy (temporary). **September and October are peak months for lead generation spend.**

### CPC Seasonality Summary

| Period | Demand | CPC | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jan-Mar | HIGHEST | Moderate | Scale aggressively. Best ROI window. |
| Apr-Jun | Moderate | HIGHEST | Focus on efficiency. Bing + retargeting. |
| Jul-Aug | Solid | Moderate | Steady spend. Content + nurture. |
| Sep-Nov | HIGH | Rising | Second scaling window. Sep is peak search volume. |
| Dec | Moderate | Softening | Holiday spend. Pipeline for January. |

### Key Mobile Stat

59% of senior care searches come from mobile devices. 62% of all advertising clicks are mobile. Mobile-first design is mandatory for landing pages and forms.
