# Senior Care Lead Buyers -- U.S. Market Research

**Compiled: 2026-03-04**

This document catalogs companies that BUY senior care leads in the United States, organized by tier. These are potential customers for a senior care lead generation business.

---

## Industry Context

- U.S. Home Care Providers industry: $155.9B market size (2025), 507K businesses
- U.S. Hospice market: ~$33B (2025)
- Senior living industry: Top 150 operators manage hundreds of thousands of units
- Average cost per lead (home care): $30-$150 depending on channel
- Average cost per lead (senior living): $20-$431+ depending on care level and market
- Customer acquisition cost (home care): $750-$1,000 per case (at 10% close rate)
- Average customer lifetime value (home care): $15,000
- No single provider controls more than 5% of the home care market -- extreme fragmentation

### Why This Tier Matters Most

- **507,000 home care businesses** in the U.S. (IBISWorld 2025)
- Most are single-location agencies with 10-50 caregivers
- They lack in-house marketing teams and rely on referrals, directories, and lead vendors
- Fastest to close -- no corporate procurement process
- Willing to pay $30-$100/lead or equivalent of first month's revenue for placement
- Best approached through local/regional marketing, industry events (Home Care 100 conference, NAHC), and vertical ad platforms

### Key States by Agency Density (target markets)

1. **Florida** -- Highest concentration of home health agencies; large elderly population

3. **California** -- Large market, strict licensing
4. **New York** -- Dense, high-value market (especially personal care/Medicaid)
5. **Georgia, Ohio, Illinois, Pennsylvania, North Carolina** -- All significant markets

### Lead Buying Behavior for Senior Living

- **Typical referral fee:** 50-100% of first month's rent ($3,000-$8,000+)
- **A Place for Mom referral:** ~equivalent to one month's rent per successful placement
- **CarePatrol referral:** 50-100% of first month's rent ($3,500-$5,000 avg.)
- **Digital leads (Google Ads):** $20-$60 per lead for standard assisted living; $100-$400+ for memory care
- **Occupancy is the #1 KPI** -- every empty bed costs $4,000-$8,000/month in lost revenue
- **Average sales cycle:** 30-90 days from lead to move-in
- **Lead-to-move-in conversion:** 3-10% depending on quality and follow-up

## TIER 5 -- Referral Platforms & Lead Marketplaces

These companies are BOTH competitors AND potential customers. They buy leads in bulk (or generate them) and resell to providers. Understanding their model helps with pricing and positioning.

### 1. A Place for Mom
- **HQ:** New York, New York
- **Type:** Online referral platform + call center with 500+ senior living advisors
- **Revenue Model:** Commission-based. Referral fee = ~one month's rent from the community when a family moves in. For a community charging $5,000/month, that is a $5,000 referral fee.
- **Revenue:** Estimated $100M+ annually
- **Reach:** 300,000+ families helped annually. Network of thousands of senior living communities and home care agencies nationwide.
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH (they are a lead BUYER at scale -- they spend heavily on Google, SEO, TV, content marketing to generate inbound leads, then monetize through referrals)
- **Notes:** The 800-lb gorilla. Under investigation by U.S. Senate for potential conflicts of interest in referral practices. Owned by General Atlantic and Silver Lake. Would be a competitor if you sell leads to communities, but a CUSTOMER if you sell them raw leads.

### 2. Caring.com (acquired by SilverAssist, Jan 2026)
- **HQ:** San Mateo, California
- **Type:** Online directory, reviews platform, and referral service
- **Revenue Model:** Multiple streams: (1) referral fees from communities (typically equal to first month's rent), (2) pay-per-lead fees, (3) annual subscription fees for enhanced listings, (4) online advertising, (5) affiliate links
- **Reach:** 375 Caring Stars agencies recognized across 45 states. Directory of ~100,000 providers.
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH (generates leads through SEO/content and monetizes them)
- **Notes:** Recently acquired by SilverAssist (Jan 2026). "Caring Stars" program is a trusted quality badge. Strong SEO presence. Potential disruption from ownership change. They are both a lead buyer and lead seller.

### 3. CarePatrol (Best Life Brands)
- **HQ:** Gilbert, Arizona
- **Type:** Senior placement advisory franchise
- **Locations:** 200+ franchise territories nationwide
- **Revenue Model:** Referral fees from communities -- typically 50-100% of first month's rent ($3,500-$5,000 avg. per placement). Free to families.
- **Avg. Franchisee Revenue:** Top performer: $2M+; system average: $346K per territory
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH (each franchisee needs leads to generate placements)
- **Notes:** Largest senior care placement franchise. Owned by Best Life Brands (also owns ComForCare). Franchisees are lead-hungry. Their model: get a lead from a family, match to community, earn referral fee. They NEED inbound family inquiries. Huge opportunity to sell them leads.

### 4. SeniorAdvisor.com
- **HQ:** Austin, Texas
- **Type:** Consumer reviews and directory site for senior care
- **Revenue Model:** Featured/premium listings, lead referrals, directory API licensing
- **Reach:** Directory of ~100,000 providers across U.S. and Canada
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Notes:** Premier reviews site. Providers pay for featured listings to appear at top of category results. API access allows other platforms to syndicate their directory data. Less aggressive lead generation than A Place for Mom but strong SEO presence.

### 5. Oasis Senior Advisors
- **HQ:** Bonita Springs, Florida
- **Type:** Senior housing referral franchise
- **Locations:** 175+ territories nationwide
- **Revenue Model:** Referral fees from communities -- typically 70-75% of monthly bed fee per successful placement
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH
- **Notes:** Uses proprietary OasisIQ matching technology. Home-based franchise, low overhead. Franchisees need family leads to generate placements. Similar model to CarePatrol but smaller. Growing footprint.

### 6. Assisted Living Locators
- **HQ:** Scottsdale, Arizona
- **Type:** Senior placement advisory franchise
- **Locations:** 150+ franchise territories
- **Revenue Model:** Referral fees from communities
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH
- **Notes:** Similar model to CarePatrol and Oasis. Each franchisee operates a territory and needs leads from families seeking assisted living, memory care, or home care placement.

### 7. Senior Care Authority
- **HQ:** Petaluma, California
- **Type:** Senior placement advisory and eldercare consulting franchise
- **Locations:** 100+ territories
- **Revenue Model:** Referral fees from communities + consulting fees from families
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH
- **Notes:** Combines placement with consulting. Dual revenue model. Franchisees buy leads for both placement and consulting services.

### 8. PayPerLead.com (and similar lead aggregators)
- **Type:** Lead marketplace / aggregator
- **Revenue Model:** Buys leads wholesale, sells to multiple providers per lead
- **Pricing:** ~$55/lead (AgingCare.com model) to $100+
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** HIGH (they are wholesale lead BUYERS)
- **Notes:** These aggregators buy or generate leads and sell them to home care agencies, senior living communities, and other providers. They are a potential customer for a lead gen business -- sell them bulk leads at wholesale.

### 9. Care.com
- **HQ:** Austin, Texas
- **Type:** Online marketplace for caregivers and families
- **Revenue Model:** Subscription fees from families AND caregivers, plus premium listings for agencies
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** MEDIUM
- **Notes:** World's largest online caregiving marketplace. 500,000+ unique visitors. 5-15% conversion on email referrals. More focused on individual caregiver matching than agency referrals, but agencies can list and buy premium placements.

### 10. Pay-Per-Call Networks Active in Senior Care
Several pay-per-call networks have senior care verticals:
- **Aragon Advertising** -- active in senior care

## TIER 6 -- Hospice & Palliative Care Providers

Hospice providers acquire patients through physician referrals, hospital discharge planners, and increasingly through direct-to-consumer marketing. Lead generation for hospice is more sensitive but represents a growing market.

### 1. VITAS Healthcare (Chemed Corporation subsidiary)

- **Locations:** 58 programs in 15 states + D.C.
- **Services:** Hospice care, palliative care
- **Revenue:** ~$1.6B+ (VITAS segment; Q4 2025 net revenue $418.8M)
- **Employees:** 12,000+
- **Average Daily Census:** 22,000+ patients
- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** MEDIUM
- **Notes:** Largest single-source hospice provider in the U.S. Publicly traded parent (CHE). Revenue growing ~6%/year. Hospice patients primarily come through physician/hospital referrals, but VITAS does consumer education marketing. Expects 5.5-6.5% revenue growth in 2026. Lead generation for hospice is less direct than home care but demand is real.

- **Lead Buying Likelihood:** MEDIUM
- **Notes:** Created from Humana's Kindred at Home hospice divestiture. CD&R purchased 60% stake for $2.8B. Divested personal care to Addus for $350M. Massive hospice footprint.
