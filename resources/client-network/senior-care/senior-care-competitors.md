# Senior Care Lead Gen -- Competitive Landscape Audit

**Date:** March 4, 2026
**Focus:** Companies generating, aggregating, and selling leads in the senior care / home care / assisted living space
**Market Context:** Senior living market growing by $130.9B from 2025-2029 (5.8% CAGR). 10,000+ Americans turn 65 daily. 54% of senior living operators say lead generation is their #1 growth priority.

---

## MARKET OVERVIEW

The senior care lead generation market operates differently from home security. In home security, the buyer is an alarm company purchasing CPL leads. In senior care, the dominant model is **referral fee on move-in** (50-120% of first month's rent, typically $2,000-$7,000+ per placement). Secondary models include pay-per-lead ($25-$55/lead for home care) and subscription-based directories.

### Key Economics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Average assisted living monthly cost | ~$4,000 |
| Average resident lifetime value | ~$124,000 (2.5-3 year avg stay) |
| Referral fee (assisted living placement) | 50-120% of first month's rent ($2,000-$7,000+) |
| Home care CPL (third-party sites) | $25-$55/lead |
| Home care client acquisition cost | $250-$1,800 |
| Home care client lifetime value | $3,000-$9,000 |
| PPC cost per lead (assisted living) | $60-$150 |
| PPC cost per lead (memory care) | $150-$400+ |
| Third-party lead conversion rate | ~2% (shared leads) |

### Market Concentration

The senior care referral market is highly concentrated:
- **A Place for Mom** dominates with the largest network (14,000+ communities) and owns AgingCare and PayingForSeniorCare
- **SilverAssist** (Caring.com + Oasis + ElderLife) is building the #2 integrated platform
- The top three ownership groups control ~34% of the top 50 websites in the market
- Franchise agencies (CarePatrol, Oasis, Senior Care Authority, Assisted Living Locators) collectively cover most US markets through local advisors

### Key Industry Dynamics

1. **Referral fee pressure.** Providers increasingly push back on paying 100%+ of first month's rent per placement. Some communities actively work to reduce dependence on aggregators.
2. **Shared vs. exclusive leads.** Major aggregators send the same family inquiry to multiple communities, driving down conversion rates (~2% reported). Exclusive lead providers are gaining traction.
3. **SilverAssist consolidation.** The January 2026 acquisition of Caring.com signals vertical integration: digital traffic (Caring.com) + local advisors (Oasis) + financing (ElderLife).
4. **AI transformation.** Platforms like Further (6,000 communities) and Aline (9,000 communities) are using AI to qualify, nurture, and convert leads, changing what providers expect from their lead sources.
5. **Regulatory scrutiny.** A Place for Mom faced a 2024 Washington Post investigation and Senate probe over recommending facilities with safety violations. Regulatory risk is rising for referral agencies.
6. **Event-based model resilience.** LeadingResponse's seminar model (3,000 events/month) generates significantly higher-quality leads than digital channels for senior living.
