# Home Security — Voice of Customer (VOC)

**Date:** March 2, 2026

---

## 1. Customer Avatar: The Home Security Buyer

**Demographics:**
- Age 27–55 (Millennials and Gen X are the primary buyers; Millennials lead at 72% adoption)
- Household income $50K–$150K+ (highest adoption at $150K+, fastest-growing interest under $50K)
- 67% homeowners, 33% renters
- 53% male, 46% female purchasers
- Concentrated in suburban and urban areas, with higher adoption in states with elevated property crime rates (CA, TX, FL, GA, IL)

**Life Situation Triggers:**
- Has children under 18 in the home (81% — the #1 purchase motivator)
- Recently moved to a new home or neighborhood
- Experienced or heard about a nearby break-in
- Travels frequently for work (32%)
- Owns high-value possessions (45%)
- Saw crime content on social media (the #1 reason people felt compelled to buy)
- Spouse/partner expressed safety concerns
- Lives alone (especially women)

**Psychographic Profile:**
- Values family safety above all; willing to pay for peace of mind
- Prefers convenience — wants a system that "just works" without technical hassle
- Increasingly tech-savvy; expects app-based control, smart home integration
- Skeptical of long contracts and hidden fees after hearing horror stories about ADT/Vivint
- Price-conscious but not cheap — will pay more for reliability and no-contract flexibility
- Wants to feel in control, not dependent on a company's response time

## 6. Objections (Why They Haven't Bought Yet)

### "It's too expensive"
- Monthly monitoring fees ($15–$53/month) add up
- Equipment costs ($200–$1,500+) are a barrier
- Fear of hidden fees and price increases
- **Reality:** Average burglary costs $5,500+ — far more than a year of monitoring

### "I'll get locked into a contract"
- Vivint's 5-year contracts and 100% cancellation fees are widely known horror stories
- ADT's difficulty-canceling reputation spreads via word of mouth
- **Counter:** No-contract options exist (SimpliSafe, Ring, Cove, Abode)

### "I can just use cameras / a Ring doorbell"
- Belief that a single camera or doorbell is "enough"
- Underestimates the value of door/window sensors, professional monitoring, and motion detection
- **Reality:** Cameras alone don't dispatch help — and most burglars enter through back doors, not the front

### "The police won't respond anyway"
- 40%+ of cities with 50K+ population won't guarantee response to residential alarms
- 95% false alarm rate erodes trust
- **Counter:** Modern systems use video verification and AI to reduce false alarms; some providers (Deep Sentinel) offer live guard intervention

### "I can protect myself / I have a dog / I have a gun"
- Overconfidence in deterrents that don't work 24/7
- A dog sleeps. A gun requires you to be home and awake.
- **Reality:** 83% of burglars check for alarm systems — visible security is the most consistent deterrent

### "It's too complicated / I'm not tech-savvy"
- Fear of installation difficulty
- Intimidated by apps, sensors, Wi-Fi setup
- **Counter:** Modern DIY systems (SimpliSafe, Ring) install in under 30 minutes with no tools

### "My neighborhood is safe"
- False sense of security based on current conditions
- 62% of people consider their homes "very safe" — until something happens
- **Reality:** Suburban homes are burglarized at nearly the same rate as urban homes

## 8. Emotional Journey Map

### Stage 1: Complacency
"My neighborhood is fine. I don't need a security system."
- Feels safe. Doesn't think about it. Dismisses security as unnecessary or paranoid.

### Stage 2: Trigger Event
"Wait — that happened HERE?"
- Sees a crime post on social media or Nextdoor. Hears about a neighbor's break-in. Has a baby. Moves to a new house. Spouse expresses worry.
- Emotional state: surprise, concern, vulnerability.

### Stage 3: Active Research / Anxiety
"What system should I get? This is overwhelming."
- Googles "best home security system." Reads Reddit threads. Compares Ring vs. SimpliSafe vs. ADT. Gets confused by contracts, pricing, features.
- Emotional state: anxious, overwhelmed, skeptical, price-sensitive.

### Stage 4: Decision / Relief
"I feel so much better knowing we're protected."
- Installs system. Sets up app. Arms it before bed. Checks camera from work.
- Emotional state: relief, control, pride ("I'm a responsible homeowner/parent").

- Lead with fear or family protection: "2,046 homes are broken into EVERY DAY. Is yours protected?"
- Use social proof: "Join 50 million+ American homes with a security system"
- Visual: nighttime footage, doorbell camera catches, family imagery

### Google Search (Intent — consideration/decision stage)
- Match high-intent queries: "best home security system no contract," "home security system cost," "SimpliSafe vs Ring"
- Lead with solution + differentiator: "No Contract. No Hidden Fees. Professional Monitoring from $15/mo."

### YouTube Pre-Roll (Interruption — awareness/consideration)
- Open with hook: real doorbell camera footage, "What would you do if you saw this on your phone?"
- Transition to benefit: peace of mind, easy setup, affordable monitoring

## 10. Halo Strategy Language (Sabri Suby Framework)

### What they SAY (surface level)
- "I'm looking for a good security system"
- "How much does ADT cost?"
- "Is Ring or SimpliSafe better?"
- "I need cameras for my house"

### What they THINK (consideration level)
- "I don't want to overpay or get scammed"
- "I need something I can actually set up myself"
- "Will this really stop someone from breaking in?"
- "I don't want a contract I can't get out of"

### What they FEEL (emotional level)
- Fear for their family's safety
- Guilt that they haven't done enough to protect their home
- Anxiety about crime in their area
- Overwhelm at the number of options and conflicting reviews

### What they truly DESIRE (identity level)
- To be a responsible, protective parent/partner/homeowner
- To feel in control of their home and family's safety
- To never be the person who says "I wish I had gotten a system sooner"
- Peace of mind — the ability to sleep soundly and leave home without worry
