# Part 1: Comparison/Review Sites

These are affiliate lead gen sites that rank gold IRA companies, collect leads via free kit/guide offers, and sell those leads to dealers. They are our direct competitors.

## 1. Gold IRA Guide (goldiraguide.org)

**Site Type:** Affiliate comparison site with aggressive lead capture
**Monetization:** Affiliate commissions from featured dealers + direct lead gen via free kit forms

### Headline and Hero Copy

- **Primary Headline:** "How will your 401k or IRA fare in The next financial crisis?"
- **Subheadline:** "Request your Free Gold IRA Kit today to learn:"
- The hero uses fear-based crisis framing, positioning gold/silver as protection against economic collapse. Lists what the kit teaches: allocation strategies, company comparisons, scam warnings, popular bullion products.

### Lead Capture Mechanism

- **Multiple email forms:** Above the fold, mid-page, and footer. Three separate placements minimum.
- **Phone number prominently displayed:** (626) 605-3152
- **Free kit download** is the primary conversion mechanic.
- **Direct company referral links** with individual phone numbers for each dealer.
- Forms appear to collect: email (required), name, potentially phone/address for kit delivery.

### CTA Copy and Button Text

| Placement | CTA Text |
|-----------|----------|
| Hero / Header / Footer | "GET YOUR KIT" |
| Per-company listings | "GET FREE KIT" |
| Footer email form | "Send My Free Guide" |
| Per-company | "Read [Company] Review" |

### Trust Signals

- **Media logos:** Bloomberg, HuffPost, Seeking Alpha, Yahoo Finance, MarketWatch ("As Seen On" bar)
- **Company rankings:** 5 top-rated companies with star ratings, individual review pages, specific phone numbers and locations
- **Author box** with bio and social links, claims "over a decade" operating history
- **IRS-Approved Depositories** referenced throughout
- No BBB badge for the site itself (only for listed companies)

### Offer Structure

**Primary:** "Free Gold IRA Kit" containing:
- Gold and silver wealth preservation education
- Allocation strategy examples
- Company comparisons/reviews
- "Top 5 Gold IRA Scams" guide
- Popular bullion coins and bars info

**Secondary:** Individual company kits from featured vendors (pass-through leads)

### Form Fields

- Email address (required, explicitly stated)
- Name (implied for kit delivery)
- Possibly phone/address (gated behind initial email)

### Follow-Up Flow

After form submission, the user likely receives:
1. Immediate email with kit download or link
2. Contact info passed to featured dealer(s)
3. Phone follow-up from sales reps at the matched company

### Design/Layout Observations

1. Header/Navigation (mega-menu)
2. Hero section with crisis headline + form CTA
3. Media credibility logos bar
4. Top 5 Gold IRA Companies (ranked, with contact info per company)
5. "Welcome to Gold IRA Guide" editorial section
6. Alternative IRA Investments section
7. Latest News/Blog posts (4 articles)
8. Popular Posts section
9. Company Reviews showcase
10. Footer form CTA (redundant capture)
11. Live price ticker (gold, silver, crypto)

**Color palette:** Gold/dark tones. Heavy on coin/vault imagery.

### Strengths

- Crisis-framing headline creates immediate emotional urgency
- Multiple form placements maximize capture opportunities
- "As Seen On" media bar adds instant credibility
- Individual company phone numbers reduce friction for phone-preference leads
- Content depth: 50+ pages of guides/reviews builds SEO authority
- "Scam" angle in kit positioning builds trust by implying the guide protects the reader

### Weaknesses

- No quiz funnel or interactive engagement. Straight form capture only.
- No video content in hero. Static page.
- Minimal urgency beyond the crisis headline. No countdown, no scarcity.
- Privacy promise ("information will not be shared or sold") may conflict with affiliate model transparency
- Disclaimer language is generic: "None of our content constitutes financial advice"
- Featured companies include some lesser-known names (GoldenCrest Metals, Silver Gold Bull) which may reduce trust vs. sites ranking only household names

### Funnel Structure: 9-Step Progressive Quiz

The funnel is a multi-step quiz that progressively qualifies the lead before capturing contact info. Each step is a single question on a clean, full-page layout. No clutter, no sidebar, no distractions.

**Step 1: "Are you interested in buying platinum or palladium?"**
- Options: Yes / No / "Not Sure Yet" (text link)
- Format: Two large icon buttons (checkmark / X)
- Purpose: Segments by metal interest. Also weeds out people who clicked by accident (if they say No to everything, they're not a real lead)

**Step 2: "How much would you consider investing in your Gold IRA as initial funding?"**
- Options: $9,999 or less / $10,000-39,999 / $40,000-69,999 / $70,000-99,999 / $100,000+
- Format: Stacked selection cards with money icons
- Purpose: THE key qualification question. This determines which dealer the lead gets routed to (dealers have different minimums). $100K+ leads are worth 5-10x what a $9,999 lead is worth.

**Step 3: "Are you investing as a couple with a partner or spouse?"**
- Options: Yes / No
- Format: Two large icon buttons
- Purpose: Spouse involvement data. Couples take longer to close but often invest more. Also useful for dealer routing (some dealers train reps specifically for couple conversations).

**Step 4: "Which of the following retirement plans do you have?" (Select all that apply)**
- Options: 401(k) / IRA/Roth / Thrift Savings Plan / Other
- Format: Checkboxes with icons, "Next" button
- Purpose: Confirms they have rollover-eligible assets. A lead with a 401(k) or IRA is worth far more than someone with just cash. TSP (Thrift Savings Plan) is military/federal employees, a specific demographic some dealers target.

**Step 5: "How closely do you monitor the stock market?"**
- Options: Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly / Randomly / "Not at all" (text link)
- Format: Stacked selection cards with calendar icons
- Purpose: Engagement level indicator. Daily/Weekly monitors are more financially anxious and closer to taking action. "Not at all" signals a passive investor who may be harder to close.

**Step 6: "Are you trying to diversify your investment portfolio?"**
- Options: Yes / No
- Format: Two large icon buttons
- Purpose: Confirms intent. A "Yes" here means they're actively looking for alternatives, not just curious. Strong buying signal.

**Step 7: "What are your goals for retirement?" (Select all that apply)**
- Options: Pursue Passions & Leisure Activities / Prioritize Family, Friends, & Wellness / Seek Adventure / Move or Increase Residences / Still Deciding / "Something Else" (text link)
- Format: Checkboxes with icons, "Next" button
- Purpose: Lifestyle data for dealer personalization. A lead who wants to "Prioritize Family" responds to legacy messaging. "Seek Adventure" responds to freedom messaging. This data helps the dealer's sales rep tailor their pitch.

**Step 8: "What is the main reason why you are interested in Gold IRA?"**
- Options: My retirement account is down / To diversify & hedge against inflation / Someone I trust recommended it / To save for my future / Distrust of traditional financial institutions / "Something else" (text link)
- Format: Stacked selection cards with icons
- Purpose: Motivation segmentation. This is incredibly valuable data for the dealer. "My retirement account is down" = urgent, fear-driven, close fast. "Distrust of traditional financial institutions" = conspiracy-adjacent, conservative messaging. "Someone I trust recommended it" = warm referral energy, easier close.

**Step 9: "Are you planning to retire in the next 20 years?"**
- Options: Yes / No / "I'm already retired" (text link)
- Subtext: "Help us understand your plans so that we can match you with a Gold IRA Company that aligns with your goals."
- Format: Two large icon buttons with text link alternative
- Purpose: Timeline qualification. "I'm already retired" is the highest-value answer (they have a lump sum sitting in a 401k/IRA right now). "Yes, within 20 years" is still good. "No" is a lower-priority lead.

**After Step 9:** Likely a contact info capture form (name, email, phone) followed by a "matching" results page showing recommended dealers.

### Design Observations

- **Ultra-clean layout.** White/light gray background, centered content, no navigation, no sidebar, no footer links. Zero distractions.
- **One question per page.** No scrolling. Each step is a single decision.
- **Consistent "GOLD IRA COMPANIES" eyebrow text** on every step. Keeps context without being pushy.
- **Large tap targets.** Every button and card is oversized. Perfect for the 55+ demographic who may be on tablets or have reduced dexterity.
- **Icon-per-option design.** Small icons on each answer choice add visual interest and make scanning easier.
- **"Not Sure Yet" / "Something Else" escape hatches.** Text links below the main options so people don't feel trapped. Smart UX that reduces abandonment.
- **No progress bar visible.** They chose not to show "Step 3 of 9." This could be intentional to prevent people from seeing how long the quiz is and dropping off early.
- **"Advertiser Disclosure" link visible at bottom** (seen in full-page screenshot). Required for FTC compliance.

### What CN Should Steal From This Funnel

1. **The investment amount question is step 2, not step 8.** They qualify on budget early so they can route leads appropriately. Don't bury this at the end.
2. **The "What is the main reason" question is pure gold for dealers.** If you pass this data along with the lead, the dealer's sales rep can open the call already knowing the prospect's motivation. That makes the lead more valuable.
3. **The retirement plan type question confirms rollover eligibility.** A lead with "401(k)" checked is immediately actionable. "Other" needs more discovery.
4. **"I'm already retired" as a separate option (not just "No") is smart.** It captures the highest-value segment distinctly.
5. **The one-question-per-page format works for this demographic.** No overwhelm, no cognitive load. Just one simple choice at a time.

### CN Funnel Ideation Notes

Jasper will need to build a similar quiz funnel. Key considerations for CN's version:

- Fewer steps than 9 (test 5-7 steps on Meta where attention spans are shorter than Google search)
- Investment amount and retirement plan type are non-negotiable qualification steps
- Motivation question adds lead value but could be optional/later in sequence
- Meta traffic may need a stronger hook page BEFORE the quiz starts (unlike Google where intent is already there)
- Phone number capture should come after the quiz "results" page, not during the quiz
- Consider adding a "Your Results" page that shows a personalized recommendation before asking for contact info (creates reciprocity)

# Gaps and Opportunities for Client Network

## What's Missing From Competitor Funnels

### 1. No Comparison Sites Use Video
Not a single comparison site has video content in their hero or above the fold. Every site is text-heavy with static images. A comparison site with a video review or explainer would immediately stand out.

### 2. No Interactive Calculators
No site offers a "Gold IRA Savings Calculator" or "Retirement Protection Calculator." Financial services sites in other verticals (mortgages, life insurance) use calculators as high-engagement lead magnets. Gold IRA has zero.

### 3. No Real-Time Price Integration on Comparison Sites
Only Gold IRA Guide shows a price ticker. No comparison site shows real-time gold/silver prices integrated into their comparison content. This is a missed engagement opportunity.

### 4. Quiz Funnels Are Underused
Only Goldco has a quiz funnel, and it's dated (2019 copyright). No comparison site uses a quiz. A "Which Gold IRA Company Is Right For You?" quiz on a comparison site would be a high-converting differentiator. Quiz funnels typically convert 30-50% higher than static forms.

### 5. No Negative/Warning Content as Lead Magnets
Augusta uses "Don't Choose the Wrong Gold Company" as exit-intent copy, but no one has built a full "Gold IRA Scams to Avoid" or "5 Red Flags in Gold IRA Companies" landing page as a primary funnel. Fear-based content converts extremely well in this demographic.

### 6. No Retargeting Infrastructure on Most Comparison Sites
IRA Empire, Gold IRA Companies Compared, and Investors Circle have no email capture at all or minimal forms. They're leaving massive retargeting value on the table. A CN comparison site with proper pixel, email capture, and retargeting sequences would dominate.

### 7. No SMS Capture on Comparison Sites
Only Birch Gold (a dealer) collects SMS consent. No comparison site captures phone numbers for SMS follow-up. Gold IRA buyers skew 55+. They check texts. SMS follow-up could be highly effective.

### 8. No Personalization
Every site serves the same content to every visitor. No one segments by investment amount, retirement timeline, or current account type (401k vs. IRA vs. cash). A personalized funnel ("Are you rolling over a 401k or funding with cash?") would increase relevance and conversion.

## Underserved Angles and Messaging

### 1. "What the Gold IRA Industry Doesn't Want You to Know"
No competitor leads with a whistleblower/insider angle. The industry has legitimate fee transparency problems (high premiums, hidden spreads). A CN funnel that exposes this while positioning itself as the honest broker would resonate with the skeptical 55+ demographic.

### 2. Political Neutrality
Goldco and Birch Gold are deeply aligned with conservative media figures. Augusta is politically neutral but positions as "educational." There's a gap for a funnel that speaks to the gold IRA buyer's economic concerns without political coding. Not everyone who wants to protect their retirement is a Hannity listener.

### 3. The "Already Talked to a Gold IRA Company" Angle
Augusta uses this briefly ("Already Talked to Another Gold IRA Company?"), but no one has built a full funnel around the second-opinion concept. Many prospects talk to Goldco or Birch first (they have the biggest ad spend), get turned off by the hard sell, and then search for alternatives. A "Second Opinion on Your Gold IRA" funnel would capture high-intent, disillusioned traffic.

### 4. Small Investor Inclusion
Augusta requires $50K minimum. Goldco requires $25K. Most comparison sites feature these companies prominently. There's an underserved market of people with $5K-$20K who want gold exposure but feel excluded by the big players. Noble Gold ($2K-$5K minimum) exists but has low visibility.

### 5. Spouse/Couple Positioning
Every funnel targets the individual. No one addresses couples making retirement decisions together. A "Protect Your Family's Retirement" or "Gold IRA for Couples" angle could unlock a different emotional register.

## Funnel Structure Recommendations for CN's Paid Media Approach

### Recommended Funnel Architecture

**Primary Funnel (Meta Ads):**
1. **Ad creative:** Fear-based or curiosity hook ("What your financial advisor won't tell you about your 401k")
2. **Landing page:** Quiz funnel. "Find the Best Gold IRA Company for Your Situation." 3-5 questions: investment amount, account type, timeline, primary concern (inflation, market crash, fees).
3. **Post-quiz:** Personalized recommendation with CTA to request a free comparison kit
4. **Lead capture:** Name, email, phone, investment amount (collected through quiz answers)
5. **Follow-up:** Immediate email with personalized report + phone call from matched dealer within 2 hours

**Secondary Funnel (Retargeting):**
1. Retarget quiz abandoners with "Don't fund a Gold IRA until you read this" angle
2. Retarget kit requesters who haven't converted with testimonials and urgency

**Content Funnel (SEO/Organic):**
1. Comparison articles optimized for "[Company A] vs [Company B]" queries
2. "Gold IRA Scams" / "Gold IRA Red Flags" content as lead magnets
3. State-specific pages ("Best Gold IRA Companies in [State]") for long-tail SEO

### Key Differentiators to Build

1. **Interactive quiz** (no comparison site has one)
2. **Fee calculator** (no one offers this)
3. **Real-time gold price integration** (engagement hook)
4. **SMS follow-up** (comparison sites don't capture phone)
5. **Politically neutral messaging** (avoid the Hannity/Bongino lane)
6. **"Second opinion" positioning** (capture disillusioned Goldco/Birch leads)
7. **Video reviews** (no comparison site uses video)

### Conversion Optimization Notes

- **Form fields:** Collect name + email + phone + investment amount range. 4 fields is the sweet spot (Birch Gold model). More than 4 and conversion drops. Fewer than 4 and lead quality suffers.
- **Trust stack:** BBB A+ (of reviewed companies) + "As Seen On" media logos + "Third-Party Verified" badge + named testimonials. This combination appears across the highest-converting sites.
- **CTA language:** Use "Claim" or "Request" over "Get" or "Download." Possessive pronouns ("Your," "My") increase conversion. Include "Free" in every primary CTA.
- **Mobile optimization:** 60%+ of gold IRA search traffic is mobile. Sticky phone CTA at top of mobile view is mandatory.
- **Exit-intent:** Deploy Augusta's model: scam/warning angle as exit popup CTA. Captures bouncing high-intent traffic.
