# 1. The Gold IRA Industry

A Gold IRA (Individual Retirement Account) is a self-directed retirement account that allows investors to hold physical precious metals (gold, silver, platinum, palladium) as qualified retirement investments instead of, or alongside, traditional paper assets like stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. Gold IRAs are governed by IRC Section 408(m) and must be administered through an IRS-approved custodian with metals stored at an approved depository.

## Core Functions

- **Wealth preservation:** Hedging retirement savings against inflation, currency devaluation, and market volatility
- **Portfolio diversification:** Adding a non-correlated asset class to traditional retirement holdings
- **Tax-advantaged precious metals ownership:** Same tax benefits as traditional or Roth IRAs (tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals)
- **Retirement rollover vehicle:** Converting existing 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), TSP, or IRA funds into physical precious metals without triggering tax events

## Primary Business Models

**Full-Service Gold IRA Companies** (Goldco, Augusta Precious Metals, Birch Gold Group, American Hartford Gold, Noble Gold)
End-to-end providers that handle account setup, custodian coordination, metals selection, purchasing, and storage logistics. They act as the customer-facing layer, partnering with custodians and depositories behind the scenes. Revenue comes from premiums charged over spot price on metals sold.

**Self-Directed IRA Custodians** (Equity Trust, STRATA Trust, Entrust Group, Pacific Premier Trust, Directed IRA/MATIC)
Financial institutions approved by the IRS to hold alternative assets in retirement accounts. They handle the administrative, compliance, and reporting side. Revenue comes from setup fees, annual maintenance fees, and transaction fees. Equity Trust alone manages 354,000+ accounts holding $81 billion in assets under custody. Pacific Premier Trust manages approximately $18 billion across 31,400 accounts.

**Precious Metals Dealers** (JM Bullion, APMEX, SD Bullion, Money Metals Exchange)
Sell IRA-eligible coins and bars. Some facilitate IRA purchases directly, others partner with custodians. Revenue is the spread between their buy price and sell price (premium over spot).

**Depositories** (Delaware Depository, Brink's Global Services, International Depository Services)
IRS-approved vaults that physically store IRA-held metals. Delaware Depository is the industry's most-used facility, approved by CME Group (COMEX/NYMEX), with $1 billion in insurance coverage and SSAE-18 audited security controls. Brink's operates facilities in Los Angeles and Salt Lake City. Revenue comes from annual storage fees.

## Industry Growth

The gold IRA industry has undergone a massive expansion. In 2014, fewer than 10 companies actively marketed gold IRA investments. By 2024, that number surged past 100. The average gold IRA investment rose from $35,000 in 2023 to $100,000 in 2024. Gold IRAs have transitioned from a niche retirement strategy into a mainstream portfolio diversification tool.

# 3. Types of Gold IRA Services

## Full-Service Gold IRA Companies

**What they do:** Handle the entire process from account opening to metals purchase and storage. Assign dedicated account representatives, coordinate with custodians and depositories, provide education, and manage ongoing account service.

**Examples:** Augusta Precious Metals, Goldco, Birch Gold Group, American Hartford Gold, Noble Gold Investments, Patriot Gold Group, Advantage Gold, Oxford Gold Group, Lear Capital, Rosland Capital, American Bullion, GoldenCrest Metals, Gold Alliance, Silver Gold Bull.

**Revenue model:** Premium over spot price on metals (typically 5-33% above spot, varying by product type and company).

## Self-Directed IRA Custodians

**What they do:** Provide the legal IRA structure, handle IRS reporting, process contributions and distributions, and hold the account assets on behalf of the investor. They don't sell metals directly.

**Examples:** Equity Trust Company, STRATA Trust Company, The Entrust Group, Pacific Premier Trust, Directed IRA (MATIC), GoldStar Trust, Kingdom Trust, Madison Trust.

**Revenue model:** Setup fees ($50-$100), annual maintenance fees ($75-$160), transaction fees.

## Precious Metals Dealers (IRA-Eligible)

**What they do:** Source and sell IRS-approved coins and bars. Some have direct custodian partnerships for seamless IRA purchases. Also serve direct-purchase (non-IRA) customers.

**Examples:** JM Bullion, APMEX, SD Bullion, Money Metals Exchange, Provident Metals, BGASC.

**Revenue model:** Buy/sell spread (premium over spot price).

## Buyback Programs

**What they do:** Purchase metals back from investors when they want to liquidate their IRA holdings or take distributions. Most major gold IRA companies offer buyback at or near spot price.

**Examples:** Goldco (guaranteed buyback), Augusta Precious Metals (transparent buyback), American Hartford Gold (no liquidation fees), Noble Gold (buyback guarantee).

**Why it matters for lead gen:** Buyback guarantees are a major selling point and conversion driver. Investors fear illiquidity.

## Storage and Depository Services

**What they do:** Physically store IRA-held precious metals in IRS-approved vaults. Provide insurance, auditing, and security. Offer segregated (your metals stored separately) or commingled (pooled) storage.

**Examples:**
- Delaware Depository (Wilmington, DE) -- industry standard, CME Group approved, $1B insurance
- Brink's Global Services (Los Angeles, CA and Salt Lake City, UT)
- International Depository Services (IDS) -- facilities in Delaware and Texas
- CNT Depository (Bridgewater, MA)
- JP Morgan Chase vault (New York)

**Revenue model:** Annual storage fees ($100-$300/year depending on account value and storage type).

# 4. Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Self-Directed IRA (SDIRA)** | An IRA where the account holder directs investments into alternative assets (real estate, precious metals, private equity, etc.) rather than being limited to stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. Requires a specialized custodian. |
| **Rollover** | Moving retirement funds from one account to another (e.g., 401(k) to Gold IRA). Indirect rollover: funds go to you first, then must be deposited within 60 days. Only one indirect rollover per 12-month period. |
| **Transfer (Direct Rollover)** | Funds move directly from one custodian to another without the account holder touching the money. No tax consequences, no 60-day deadline, no limit on frequency. The preferred method. |
| **Custodian** | An IRS-approved financial institution that holds and administers the IRA. Required by law. The custodian handles reporting, compliance, and record-keeping. Cannot be the investor themselves. |
| **Depository** | An IRS-approved secure storage facility where physical precious metals are held. Must be a bank, approved non-bank trustee, or other approved entity. The investor cannot store metals at home or in a personal safe deposit box. |
| **IRS-Approved Metals** | Specific coins and bullion that meet IRC Section 408(m)(3) purity requirements. Not all gold products qualify. The IRS maintains strict fineness standards. |
| **Fineness Requirements** | Minimum purity standards: Gold 99.5% (0.995), Silver 99.9% (0.999), Platinum 99.95% (0.9995), Palladium 99.95% (0.9995). Exception: American Gold Eagle (91.67% pure / 22 karat) is approved because it is minted and guaranteed by the U.S. government. |
| **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)** | Mandatory annual withdrawals from traditional IRAs starting at age 73 (post-SECURE 2.0 Act). Gold IRA holders must either sell metals to cover RMDs or take an in-kind distribution of physical metals. Roth IRAs are exempt from RMDs during the owner's lifetime. |
| **Prohibited Transactions** | Actions the IRS forbids within an IRA, including self-dealing, using IRA assets for personal benefit, or transacting with disqualified persons (family members, fiduciaries). Violations result in the entire IRA being treated as a distribution, triggering taxes and penalties. |
| **Home Storage (Illegal)** | Storing IRA-owned precious metals at your home, in a personal safe, or in a standard bank safe deposit box. This is a distribution, not storage. Triggers income tax on the full value plus a 10% early withdrawal penalty if under 59.5. Some companies have marketed "home storage IRAs" -- the IRS and courts have consistently ruled against this. |
| **Precious Metals (IRA-Eligible)** | Four metals allowed: Gold, Silver, Platinum, Palladium. Must be in the form of IRS-approved coins or bars from accredited refiners/mints. |
| **Proof Coins vs. Bullion** | Proof coins are specially minted with mirror-like finishes, sold at higher premiums (collectible appeal). Bullion coins/bars are standard investment-grade products sold closer to spot price. Both can be IRA-eligible if they meet purity requirements, but bullion typically offers better value for IRA investors. |
| **Spot Price** | The current market price for immediate delivery of one troy ounce of a precious metal. Changes constantly during trading hours. The baseline price before any dealer premium. |
| **Premium Over Spot** | The amount a dealer charges above the spot price. Covers minting costs, dealer margin, and distribution. Ranges from 3-8% for bullion bars to 10-33%+ for proof coins. Lower premiums generally mean better value for the investor. |

# 8. Regulatory Landscape

## IRS Rules (IRC Section 408(m))

**Collectibles Rule:** IRC Section 408(m) generally treats the acquisition of any collectible by an IRA as a taxable distribution. Precious metals are classified as collectibles UNLESS they meet specific exceptions.

**Approved Metals and Fineness:**

| Metal | Minimum Fineness | Common IRA-Eligible Products |
|---|---|---|
| Gold | 99.5% (0.995) | American Gold Eagle* (exception at 91.67%), Canadian Maple Leaf, Australian Kangaroo, Austrian Philharmonic, PAMP Suisse bars, Credit Suisse bars |
| Silver | 99.9% (0.999) | American Silver Eagle, Canadian Silver Maple Leaf, Austrian Silver Philharmonic |
| Platinum | 99.95% (0.9995) | American Platinum Eagle, Canadian Platinum Maple Leaf |
| Palladium | 99.95% (0.9995) | Canadian Palladium Maple Leaf |

*The American Gold Eagle is the only exception to the fineness rule because it is produced by the U.S. Mint and its weight, content, and purity are guaranteed by the federal government.

**NOT Allowed:** South African Krugerrands (only 91.67% pure, not a U.S. government coin), pre-1933 gold coins, most numismatic/collectible coins, jewelry, any coin or bar below fineness thresholds.

## Custodian Requirements

- All IRA assets must be held by an IRS-approved custodian (bank, savings association, or entity approved by the IRS under IRC Section 408(a)).
- The custodian handles tax reporting (Form 5498, 1099-R), contribution tracking, and RMD calculations.
- The investor cannot serve as their own custodian.

## Storage Rules

- Physical precious metals must be stored at an IRS-approved depository. Not at home, not in a personal safe, not in a standard bank safe deposit box.
- "Home storage IRA" schemes have been consistently struck down by the IRS and Tax Court. In McNulty v. Commissioner (2021), the Tax Court ruled that storing IRA-owned metals at home constitutes a distribution.
- Segregated storage (your metals stored separately, identifiable by serial numbers) is available at a premium. Commingled storage (pooled with other clients' metals of the same type) is cheaper.

## RMD Rules

- Traditional Gold IRA holders must begin taking Required Minimum Distributions at age 73 (under SECURE 2.0 Act, effective 2023).
- RMDs can be satisfied by: (1) selling metals and distributing cash, or (2) taking an in-kind distribution of physical metals (the metal's fair market value counts toward the RMD).
- Failure to take RMDs results in a 25% excise tax on the shortfall (reduced from 50% by SECURE 2.0).
- Roth Gold IRAs are exempt from RMDs during the original owner's lifetime.

## Tax Implications

- **Traditional Gold IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible. Growth is tax-deferred. Distributions are taxed as ordinary income.
- **Roth Gold IRA:** Contributions are after-tax. Growth and qualified distributions are tax-free.
- **Early withdrawal (before 59.5):** 10% penalty plus income tax on the distribution amount.
- **Collectibles treatment:** If you buy a non-qualifying metal in your IRA, the purchase amount is treated as a distribution immediately. Income tax plus 10% penalty if under 59.5.

## FTC Advertising Rules

- The FTC's Guides for the Jewelry, Precious Metals, and Pewter Industries (16 CFR Part 23) govern advertising claims about precious metals.
- Companies cannot misrepresent the purity, weight, or composition of metals.
- All claims about gold content, purity, and fineness must be substantiated and accurately disclosed.
- The FTC has taken enforcement action against fraudulent gold/silver dealers for misrepresenting products and using deceptive advertising (e.g., FTC v. Park Avenue Partners LLC, 2011; FTC v. Discount Gold Brokers, 2016).
- Advertising gold as a "guaranteed" investment or making income claims without substantiation violates FTC Act Section 5.

## State Regulations

- Some states require precious metals dealers to register or obtain specific licenses.
- State consumer protection laws apply in addition to federal FTC rules.
- State securities regulators may classify certain gold investment products as securities, requiring additional compliance.
