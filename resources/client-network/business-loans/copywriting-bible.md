## 2. Facebook Ad Hooks

### Fear / Under the Fingernail (Cash Flow Crisis, MCA Trap, Rejection)

1. 82% of small businesses that fail have one thing in common: a cash flow problem they waited too long to solve.
2. He took an MCA to make payroll. Six months later, he'd lost his employees, his savings, and his lease. That's how fast it happens.
3. Payroll is due Friday. Your biggest client just pushed their invoice to "net 60." The bank said they'd get back to you in six to eight weeks.
4. Every month you're robbing Peter to pay Paul, paying one vendor late so you can make payroll, then falling behind on rent. There's a better way to fund a business.
5. You didn't start a business to spend every night lying awake doing mental math about whether you can survive another month.
6. "One MCA turned into two, then three. I was borrowing from Peter to pay Paul, and then Paul sued me." If that sounds familiar, read this before you take another advance.

### Identity / Builder's Pride (You Built This, You Deserve Better)

1. You didn't build this business from nothing just to beg a bank for permission to grow it.
2. You're a plumber, a contractor, a restaurant owner, not a financial analyst. You shouldn't need to be one just to get a fair loan.
3. You've spent years building something real. The only thing standing between where you are and where you're going is capital.
4. You started this business so you could be your own boss. Don't let a bad lending decision put someone else in control.
5. Behind every "small" business is someone who took a massive risk, built something from scratch, and just needs the capital to take it to the next level.
6. You didn't quit your job and bet on yourself just to be told "insufficient credit history" by a bank that has never met a payroll.

### Simplicity / Speed (Fast Funding, Easy Process)

1. One application. Multiple lender options. A clear answer in minutes, not months.
2. SBA loans take 60 to 90 days. Your business can't wait that long. See what you qualify for in 60 seconds.
3. Forget the 40-page application. Forget the six-week wait. Business funding doesn't have to be this hard.
4. Stop Googling "how to get a business loan" and get a real answer. It takes 60 seconds and won't affect your credit.
5. Matched with a lender. Approved. Funded. Some business owners complete the entire process in 48 hours.
6. You need capital, not a second job filling out paperwork. See your options in minutes.

### Affordability / Cost Comparison (Price Anchoring)

1. MCA lenders charge effective rates of 35% to 350%. A well-matched term loan can cost a fraction of that. Know the difference before you sign anything.
2. Think business loans are too expensive right now? The cost of NOT borrowing is often higher: missed contracts, lost employees, emergency financing at triple the rate.
3. An MCA that seems like "fast cash" can cost you $1.35 to $1.50 for every dollar you borrow. A structured term loan might cost you 20 cents. The math matters.
4. 51% of business owners say they can't afford a loan at current rates. But most haven't seen what they actually qualify for. The gap between what you assume and what's available might surprise you.

### Skepticism / Trust (Broker Distrust, Predatory Lending)

1. Getting 15 calls a day from "business loan brokers" you never contacted? You're not alone. Here's how to tell a real lender from a scam.
2. "Every broker who calls me guarantees approval. Legitimate lenders don't guarantee anything." If you're tired of the runaround, try something different.
3. No upfront fees. No daily withdrawals. No confessions of judgment. No hidden costs. Just a clear comparison of what you actually qualify for.
4. "Banks make you beg for money and speak down to you." You deserve a funding partner who explains everything in plain English and works for you, not against you.
5. The business lending industry has a trust problem, and it's earned. Here's how to get capital without getting taken advantage of.
6. Before you sign anything, ask three questions: What is the total cost of this loan? Are there daily deductions? Is there a confession of judgment clause? If they can't answer clearly, walk away.

### Urgency / News (Rate Changes, Seasonal Demand, New Programs)

1. The Fed cut rates three times in 2025. Business loan rates are shifting. If you've been waiting for a better time to borrow, this is it.
2. 45% of small businesses applied for financing this year, the highest rate since 2021. Lender capacity fills up fast. Don't wait until your options narrow.
3. New SBA rules just doubled the loan cap for manufacturers to $10 million. If you're in manufacturing, this changes what's possible for your business.
4. Commercial bankruptcy filings hit 31,810 this year, up 5%. Most of those businesses had a solvable cash flow problem that became a crisis because they waited too long.

### Specificity / Stats (Data-Driven Hooks)

1. 82% of businesses that fail cite cash flow as the cause. 39% don't have enough cash to cover a single month of expenses. Which side of that line is your business on?
2. Banks deny over 80% of small business loan applications. That doesn't mean you don't qualify. It means you applied to the wrong lender.
3. 44% of small business owners don't even apply for funding because they expect rejection. Over 2 million businesses every year leave money on the table.
4. Nearly 1 in 4 business owners who get denied don't even know why. A 60-second pre-qualification can show you exactly where you stand.
5. Only 41% of loan applicants receive the full amount they request. The businesses that get fully funded have one thing in common: they matched with the right lender type first.

### Social Proof (Success Stories Framing)

1. "After being turned down by three banks, I was starting to lose hope. Then I got matched with a lender who approved me in 48 hours." That's the difference the right match makes.
2. "Application process was easy. Financing within one day." Business funding doesn't have to be a nightmare.
3. "He explained everything in exact detail, notified me on every step." That's what it feels like to work with someone who's actually on your side.
4. "I wish I'd known about this six months ago." That's what business owners say after they stop Googling and get matched with the right lender.

## 11. Under-the-Fingernail Copy Blocks

**Block 1: The Payroll Friday**

It's Thursday afternoon. Payroll hits tomorrow. You've been watching the bank balance all week, refreshing the screen like it might change if you look at it enough. It won't. Your biggest client owes you $47,000 and their AP department says the check will go out "next week." You've heard that before. Your three employees don't know any of this. They show up every morning, do good work, trust that the check clears on Friday like it always does. You've never missed one. But you're doing math in your head that nobody else sees. If you transfer from savings, that covers payroll, but then the rent on your shop is short. If you delay the materials order, you lose the discount and push the next job back two weeks. If you call your vendor and ask for another extension, that's the third time this year and you can feel the relationship fraying. Your wife asks you at dinner why you're so quiet. You say you're tired. You're not tired. You're terrified. Because you've spent six years building this business, hired people who depend on you, and right now, at 9:47 on a Thursday night, you're staring at a number on a screen and wondering if this is the week it all falls apart. Not because the business isn't good. The business is good. The pipeline is full. The work is there. But the cash isn't, because someone else decides when they feel like paying you, and the entire weight of that timing gap falls on your shoulders and no one else's.

**Block 2: The MCA Spiral**

He needed $45,000 to hire two technicians and buy a truck for his growing HVAC business. Revenue was up. Bookings were strong. The only problem was timing: the money was going out faster than it was coming in, and the seasonal push was two months away. He Googled "fast business loan." The first three results were MCA companies. One of them funded him in 48 hours. He signed the contract at midnight at his kitchen table because payroll was due Monday. The factor rate was 1.35, which sounded fine because nobody explained what that actually meant in dollars. What it meant was $60,750 in total repayment on a $45,000 advance, withdrawn daily from his business checking account. Every morning, before his first employee clocked in, $487 disappeared. When a client paid late, the account dipped below zero and the bank charged overdraft fees on top of the MCA deductions. Within three months, he was behind on rent. He cut his marketing budget to zero. He let one of the technicians go, the same person he'd hired with the money. By month five, a second MCA company called offering to "consolidate" his first advance into a new one. It sounded like relief. It was another $50,000 at a 1.40 factor rate stacked on top of the first. Now he had two sets of daily withdrawals hitting his account. He was dodging calls from the landlord, the insurance company, and a collections team that had started emailing his clients directly to pressure him into paying. His wife was seven months pregnant. His accountant told him the only option left was bankruptcy. The business that was supposed to be his family's future had become the thing destroying it, not because the work wasn't there, but because he signed one contract at midnight that he didn't fully understand, and nobody on the other side of that contract had any interest in whether his business survived.

**Block 3: The Discouraged Borrower**

She has been running her catering company for four years. Revenue hit $380,000 last year, up from $210,000 the year before. But she needs $75,000 to buy a delivery van, upgrade her equipment, and hire a prep cook so she can take on the corporate contracts that keep coming in.
