# Criminal Defense / DUI Lead Buyers: U.S. Market Research

**Compiled:** 2026-04-04

This document catalogs companies that BUY criminal defense and DUI leads in the United States, organized by tier. These are potential customers for a criminal defense lead generation business.

---

## Industry Context

- U.S. legal services market: $396.8B (2024), growing at 2.5% CAGR
- Criminal defense consulting services: $2.2B (2024), growing at 6.72% CAGR
- 252,159+ criminal defense lawyers currently employed in the U.S.
- 804,926 DUI arrests in 2024 (each generating immediate attorney demand)
- Average DUI attorney retainer: $1,500-$5,000 (first offense), $4,000-$10,000+ (repeat/felony)
- Criminal defense leads sell at $50-$300/lead depending on case type, exclusivity, and geography
- Google Ads CPCs for DUI keywords: $50-$150+ per click in major metros
- Google LSA criminal defense leads average approximately $111/lead
- No single firm controls more than 1% of the criminal defense market: extreme fragmentation creates massive PPL opportunity

## Strategic Notes

**Intermediaries (Tier 5) are the fastest path to revenue.** A single wholesale agreement with 4LegalLeads, Legal Brand Marketing, or Best Case Leads immediately activates their entire buyer network without requiring individual firm relationships. They already have attorneys waiting for criminal defense lead supply.

**Tier 1 and Tier 2 volume firms offer higher CPLs but longer sales cycles** because you are selling directly to the law firm's marketing director or managing partner.

**The NACDL/NCDD Las Vegas conference in October 2026 is the single highest-leverage event.** 600+ DUI attorneys networking explicitly to build referral relationships. A booth or sponsorship puts the lead product directly in front of the full buyer pool in one concentrated effort.

**Prepaid legal networks (LegalShield, ARAG, U.S. LawShield) are indirect opportunities.** Their network attorneys receive fixed-fee plan work but need retainer-paying criminal defense cases to supplement their income, making them receptive to PPL.
