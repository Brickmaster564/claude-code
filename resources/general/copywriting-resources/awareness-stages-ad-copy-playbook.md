# Awareness Stages Ad Copy Playbook

Source reference:
- file_33 image, "The 5 Stages of Awareness"

Purpose:
- Use this as a fast decision guide when writing ad copy.
- Match message to awareness stage before writing headlines, hooks, body copy, and CTA.

## The 5 stages and what to write

### 1) Unaware
State:
- Prospect does not clearly acknowledge the problem yet.

Copy objective:
- Create recognition and emotional resonance.
- Surface hidden pain, risk, or missed opportunity.

Message style:
- Pattern interrupt
- Story, contrast, future consequence
- Soft CTA, low-friction next step

Do:
- Lead with relatable moments and symptoms.
- Name the cost of inaction in real-life terms.

Avoid:
- Hard product pitch too early.
- Technical explanations.

CTA examples:
- Learn more
- See why this happens
- Find out what most people miss

---

### 2) Problem Aware
State:
- Prospect knows they have a problem, but does not know clear solutions.

Copy objective:
- Deepen problem clarity.
- Build urgency and desire to solve now.

Message style:
- Problem amplification
- Myth-breaking
- Early reframing

Do:
- Show why the problem persists.
- Make the pain specific and measurable.

Avoid:
- Jumping to detailed product claims too quickly.

CTA examples:
- See your options
- Get the guide
- Learn the next step

---

### 3) Solution Aware
State:
- Prospect knows solutions exist, but not specific products.

Copy objective:
- Position the mechanism and approach.
- Differentiate category options.

Message style:
- Mechanism-led education
- Comparison logic
- Objection pre-handling

Do:
- Explain how your approach works in plain language.
- Address why common alternatives fail.

Avoid:
- Feature dumps without relevance.

CTA examples:
- Compare solutions
- See how this works
- Check if this fits you

---

### 4) Product Aware
State:
- Prospect knows your product exists, but is unconvinced.

Copy objective:
- Build belief and reduce risk.
- Prove outcome reliability.

Message style:
- Proof-heavy
- Risk-reversal
- Specific offer clarity

Do:
- Use case studies, testimonials, and concrete stats.
- Handle top objections directly.

Avoid:
- Generic brand language.
- Vague claims without evidence.

CTA examples:
- Get your quote
- Start your trial
- Book your call

---

### 5) Most Aware
State:
- Prospect knows your product and what it does, but has not purchased yet.

Copy objective:
- Remove final friction.
- Trigger immediate action.

Message style:
- Offer-driven
- Scarcity or deadline
- Guarantee and certainty

Do:
- Restate core outcome briefly.
- Emphasize urgency and low-risk action.

Avoid:
- Over-educating.
- Long setup copy.

CTA examples:
- Claim your spot
- Activate now
- Lock in today

## Ad ideation workflow by stage

1) Identify stage before writing.
2) Pick one core job for the ad at that stage.
3) Write hook, body, and CTA only for that job.
4) Validate copy against stage-specific checklist.
5) Launch variants by angle, not random rewrites.

## Life insurance application notes

Unaware:
- Focus on family consequence and responsibility moments.

Problem Aware:
- Highlight financial exposure, mortgage, childcare, income dependency.

Solution Aware:
- Clarify term-life logic, affordability, and process simplicity.

Product Aware:
- Prove exclusivity, speed, OTP verification, and trust markers.

Most Aware:
- Push quote completion with urgency, clarity, and risk reversal.

## Operating rule

Never write one generic ad for all audiences.
Build stage-specific variants, then test and scale by stage performance.
