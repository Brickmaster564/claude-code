# Universal Headlines Swipe File DNA

Structural patterns extracted from proven winning headlines. Apply across all verticals and channels.

**Source:** Jasper's live campaign headlines across Client Network verticals.

---

## Core Rule: Headlines Are Assemblies, Not Inventions

Winning headlines are built from modular components snapped together. They are NOT creative writing. Every headline is a combination of:

**[Audience] + [Benefit/Offer] + [CTA Hook]**

The skill is picking the right components and ordering them for maximum impact.

---

## The 6 Headline Structures

### 1. Audience Call-Out + Benefit + CTA Hook
The workhorse. Works for every vertical, every awareness stage.

**Structure:** `[Who]: [What they get] ([Next step])`

**Examples:**
- "Americans: Up To $2M Life Insurance Cover Today (FREE Quote)"
- "[Homeowners]: Up To [Coverage] [Product] Today (Free Quote)"

**Variables:**
- Audience: demographic, geo, life stage, role
- Benefit: coverage amount, savings, outcome
- CTA hook: Free Quote / See If You Qualify / In Minutes

**When to use:** Default structure. Start here, test away from here.

---

### 2. Objection Flip + Proof
Names the #1 reason they haven't acted, then destroys it with a specific proof point.

**Structure:** `[Objection as question]? [Proof that kills it].`

**Examples:**
- "Think Life Insurance is Expensive? ...Less Than a Daily Coffee."
- "Think [Product] is [Objection]? [Audience] are getting [Benefit] for [Tiny Price]."

**Variables:**
- Objection: too expensive, too complicated, takes too long, don't trust it
- Proof: price anchor, speed, simplicity claim, social proof number

**When to use:** Problem-aware and solution-aware audiences who have a specific blocker.

---

### 3. Stacked Proof Points
No fluff. Just the 3 most compelling facts, rapid-fire.

**Structure:** `[Big number]. [Objection killer]. [Speed/ease].`

**Examples:**
- "$2M Cover. No Medical. 60 Seconds."
- "[Coverage/Benefit]. [Removed friction]. [Time to complete]."

**Variables:**
- Lead number: coverage amount, savings amount, number of people helped
- Friction killer: no exam, no phone calls, no waiting periods
- Speed: 60 seconds, 2 minutes, in minutes, today

**When to use:** Product-aware and most-aware audiences. They know what they want — hit them with proof.

---

### 4. Audience + Benefit + Price Anchor
Qualifies the reader, states the benefit, anchors to a trivially small price.

**Structure:** `[Product] For [Audience] — [Benefit] From [Tiny Price]`

**Examples:**
- "Life Insurance For Parents Over 30 — Up To $2M Coverage From $1/Day"
- "[Product] For [Audience] — Up To [Coverage] From [Price Anchor]"

**Variables:**
- Audience qualifier: age, role, location, life stage
- Benefit: coverage amount, savings, outcome
- Price anchor: $1/day, less than a coffee, pennies a day

**When to use:** Cold traffic where affordability is the main objection.

---

### 5. Geo + Offer
Hyper-local targeting. The state/city name acts as a pattern interrupt in the feed.

**Structure:** `[Adjective] [Product] — [Benefit] In [Location]`

**Examples:**
- "Cheap Life Insurance — Up To $2M Cover In Virginia"
- "[Adjective] [Product] — [Benefit] In [State/City]"

**Variables:**
- Location: state, city, region — swap per ad set
- Adjective: cheap, affordable, fast, simple
- Benefit: coverage amount, price point

**When to use:** Geo-targeted campaigns. The location name stops the scroll because it feels personal.

---

### 6. Pain/Confusion + Resolution
Names the emotional blocker, then promises it's solved.

**Structure:** `Finally, [Product] Without the [Pain]. [Proof of ease].`

**Examples:**
- "Finally, Life Insurance Without the Confusion. Most American families get covered in under 10 minutes."
- "Finally, [Product] Without the [Pain Point]. [Social proof or speed claim]."

**Variables:**
- Pain: confusion, hassle, phone calls, paperwork, pushy agents
- Resolution: speed claim, simplicity proof, social proof

**When to use:** Problem-aware audiences whose main blocker is the process, not the product.

## Variable Banks — Swap & Test

These are the modular components. Mix and match across structures.

### Audience Qualifiers
| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Demographic | Americans, Parents Over 30, Ages 30-60 |
| Role | Homeowners, Main Earners, New Parents |
| Geo | [State], [City], [Region] |
| Life stage | Just Had a Baby, Just Bought a Home, Recently Married |

### Benefit Anchors
| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| Coverage amount | $100K / $250K / $500K / $1M / $2M |
| Price anchor | $1/day / less than a coffee / pennies a day |
| Speed | 60 seconds / 2 minutes / in minutes / today |
| Simplicity | No medical / No phone calls / No paperwork / From home |

### CTA Hooks (parenthetical or trailing)
| Hook | When to use |
|------|-----------|
| (FREE Quote) | Default — lowest friction |
| (See If You Qualify) | Creates curiosity + exclusivity |
| (Get Covered Today) | Urgency, most-aware |
| (In Minutes) | Speed-focused, simplicity angle |
| (60 Seconds) | Extreme speed proof |

## Headline Testing Rules

1. **Test structure first, variables second.** Don't test "Americans" vs "Parents Over 30" until you know if Structure 1 beats Structure 3.
2. **One variable change per test.** If you change the audience AND the benefit, you learn nothing.
3. **Lead with the biggest number.** $2M beats $500K in headlines even if most people qualify for less. The number stops the scroll.
4. **Parenthetical CTAs work.** "(FREE Quote)" at the end performs. Don't overthink it.
5. **Geo headlines are cheap wins.** Same headline, swap the state per ad set. Easy volume.

## Applying This DNA to New Verticals

When writing headlines for a new vertical, fill in these slots:

1. **Audience:** Who are they? (demographic, role, geo, life stage)
2. **Product:** What is it in plain language?
3. **Big number:** What's the most impressive coverage/savings/outcome number?
4. **Price anchor:** What's the trivially cheap comparison?
5. **Speed proof:** How fast can they get started/complete the process?
6. **Friction killer:** What's the thing they expect to hate that you've removed?
7. **#1 Objection:** What's the main reason they haven't acted?
8. **Geo slots:** Which states/cities are you targeting?

Then run each through the 6 structures above. You'll have 6+ headline variants in minutes.
