## Core principles absorbed

1) Advertising is salesmanship, not decoration
- Primary test is whether it sells.
- Creativity is a means, not the end.

2) Timeless principles persist across channels
- Media evolves, core persuasion drivers stay stable.
- New channels should inherit proven direct-response logic.

3) Research-driven decisions beat taste-based decisions
- Strong campaigns come from disciplined research and testing.
- Assumptions should be replaced by evidence from market response.

4) Clarity and specificity outperform vague claims
- The prospect should instantly understand what is offered and why it matters.
- Information-rich copy generally outperforms empty cleverness.

5) Strong headlines remain the highest-leverage line of copy
- Headline determines whether body copy gets consumed.
- Benefit-led, specific, relevance-first framing is critical.

6) Long copy can win when buying friction is high
- If decision complexity and skepticism are high, depth converts.
- Copy should be as long as necessary to answer objections and prove value.

7) Direct-response structure is transferable
- The same fundamentals can be adapted across print, TV, direct mail, and digital funnels.

8) Agency and client outcomes depend on commercial discipline
- Winning work aligns messaging, offer, and measurable business outcomes.

## How this applies to our work

### Client Network
- Keep all messaging sales-first and proof-led.
- Tighten headlines around true-cost-per-sale logic.
- Use clear structure to handle skepticism early.
- Prioritise testable claims, proof density, and objection coverage.

### Nalu
- Position as commercial growth infrastructure, not "content activity".
- Use direct-response discipline in positioning, offer pages, and outreach.
- Tie thought leadership assets to measurable conversion events.

## Operator rule from this reference
- If copy cannot clearly explain value and move a measurable action, rewrite it.
