# Meta Campaign Playbook — Running Aggressive Campaigns

Book II from Uproas "The Complete Playbook of High-Risk Facebook Advertising" by Lukas Lainsalu.

Core philosophy: **Aggression + Control = Longevity.** Anyone can launch risky ads. The difference is whether they last 6 hours or 6 months. You are not trying to outsmart Meta. You are learning how to operate in its blind.

Last updated: 2026-03-10

---

## Chapter 1: Warm-Up System

Before launching aggressive traffic, you must build trust into the account.

### Neutral Ad Buffer Method

- For every 1 aggressive ad, run 4-5 neutral ads
- Neutral ads run at $1-$2/day
- Use low-risk verticals: gadgets, beauty, pets, lifestyle, accessories
- Keep creatives simple: static image + headline + CTA
- Purpose: inflates approval ratio and builds a healthy ad history signal

### Duplication Strategy

- Duplicate "whitehat ads" frequently to inflate approval ratio
- Make small edits (different image or CTA) when duplicating — signals testing variations to the system
- This raises your approval percentage and builds a positive account history pattern

### Activity Rotation

- Never repeat the same audience, GEO, or interest targeting on neutral ads
- Rotate every 48 hours
- Creates the appearance of genuine advertiser testing activity

## Chapter 2: Launching Aggressive Campaigns

### Step 1: Minimal Spend Testing

- Start at $2-$4 daily budget per ad
- Goal: confirm the ad spends without triggering review issues
- Once delivering normally, duplicate and increase budget safely
- If a creative doesn't spend or gets rejected: do NOT appeal. Duplicate with small changes instead.
- Appealing creates a permanent review flag. Never appeal a rejected creative.

### Step 2: Android/Emulator Logins (Optional)

- Logging in through Android devices or emulators can improve approval rates
- Reduces random verification prompts
- Not mandatory. Skip if you are managing a single environment.

### Step 3: Timing Windows

Best times to submit ads for review:
- **Late night: 11 PM – 2 AM** in the account's local timezone
- **Early morning: 5 – 7 AM** in the account's local timezone

Why: less human moderation active during these windows. Creatives slip through automated review faster.

### Step 4: Layered Upload Technique

If an ad is getting rejected, upload it in layers:
1. Upload the background only first
2. Add text
3. Add sound or voiceover
4. Overlay on a clean background clip and reduce brightness slightly

Each layer passes through review incrementally rather than being evaluated as a complete unit.

### Step 5: Objective Switching

- Launch first using the **Video Views** objective (faster approval, less scrutiny)
- After the ad is approved and spending, duplicate the campaign
- Switch the duplicate to **Website Traffic** or **Conversions**
- This isolates whether rejections come from the creative itself or the landing page

## Chapter 3: Scaling Without Detection

**Core principle: Meta does not punish spending. It punishes unnatural behavior.**

### Smart Budget Framework

- Start conversion campaigns at **$49/day**
- After 48 hours of stable performance: scale to **$99/day**
- Beyond $100/day: split across multiple ad accounts (optional — some maintain a single account if stable)

### Campaign Separation

- Keep **CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization)** and **BID (manual bid)** campaigns on separate ad accounts
- Never mix both strategies in the same account

### Smart Scaling Rules

- Increase budgets by no more than **20-30% per day**
- Avoid editing live campaigns. Duplicate instead.
- Watch for CPM spikes or CTR drops — these are early warning signs of account stress

## Chapter 4: Creative Rotation and Pattern Breaking

Meta detects repetitive ad patterns faster than any human reviewer. Pattern recognition is the primary detection mechanism.

### Rotation Formula

- Change one element every 3-5 days: image, CTA, or copy
- Maintain a library of at least 20 creatives
- Always keep neutral ads running in the background

### Ad Health Tracking

Monitor these three metrics continuously:
- **Approval ratio** (approved vs. rejected ads)
- **CPM trend lines** (rising CPMs = account stress)
- **Page feedback score**

If any of these drops sharply: rotate creatives immediately or pause risky campaigns for 24 hours.

## Chapter 5: Crisis Protocol

When an account gets restricted, the sequence matters. Do this exactly:

1. Pause all ads immediately
2. **Do not appeal.** Appealing creates a permanent review flag.
3. Request a new Execution BM (preferably brand new)
4. Get a new agency ad account
5. Reconnect Pixel and Profiles from Safe BM
6. Relaunch campaigns with the same creatives and data

Outcome: Pixel and Page remain untouched. Recovery in hours, not days.

## Chapter 6: 7-Day Aggression Plan

The goal of the first 7 days is to make the BM look like a legitimate testing environment with healthy activity.

**Days 1-2:** Run only neutral ads at $1-2/day across 4-5 low-risk creatives. No aggressive content yet. Build the activity log.

**Days 3-4:** Introduce your first aggressive creative at $2-4/day alongside the neutral buffer. Use late-night submission timing. Use Video Views objective.

**Days 5-6:** If the aggressive creative is spending normally, duplicate it. Increase budget to $9-15/day on the duplicate. Keep the original running. Swap the duplicate to Website Traffic objective.

**Day 7:** Scale the performing duplicate to $49/day. Rotate one neutral creative to stay fresh. At this point the account has a full week of diversified ad behavior and a healthy approval ratio.

After one week: the BM appears as a legitimate testing environment. You are now operating from a position of earned trust.

## Operating Philosophy

- Warm up before every aggressive push
- Test at minimal spend, duplicate to scale
- Submit during low-moderation windows
- Use the layered upload technique for borderline creatives
- Never appeal rejections — duplicate and adjust instead
- Change one creative element every 3-5 days
- Duplicate instead of editing live campaigns
- Watch CPM and approval ratio as your primary health indicators
- On restriction: pause, replace BM, reconnect pixel, relaunch. No recovery drama.
