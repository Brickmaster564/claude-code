# Meta Ad Platform Recovery SOP

Playbook for handling every type of Meta restriction or ban. The core principle: have backups in place before something bad happens. Recovery is much easier when you are not scrambling.

Source: Internal SOP document.
Last updated: 2026-03-10

---

## Backup Infrastructure to Maintain at All Times

Before anything breaks, you should have this ready:

- **2-4 aged US Facebook personal profiles**
- **5+ Business Managers** with no budget or ad account restrictions (ideally all verified, 2-3 admins each)
- **15-25+ Facebook pages** with 500-1,500 likes, enough content, aged
- **5-10+ Ad accounts per BM**

Support contacts:
- **FB Support:** https://www.facebook.com/business/help/support

---

## Restriction: Personal Profile

1. Reach out to Facebook Support via both chat and email
2. Explain the reason your profile may have been restricted (traveling, IP address, business in another country, etc.)
   - Use ChatGPT to help draft the message
3. Provide: ID, payment debit card associated with the account, email
4. **Buy 2 USA Facebook profiles through a reseller** (search Google for sellers)
5. Log into the profiles through a remote desktop with a static IP VPN
6. Warm up the profile — check Facebook a couple of days in a row until you start seeing ads in the feed
7. Add yourself into the Business Manager (if it is not disabled)
8. Buy a new verified BM with a $250 limit or no limit
9. Reach out to family and friends for temporary access
   - Use remote desktop and VPN on their profiles
   - Ask them not to use the profile until you get another one
10. Quickly hire an external media buyer who has access to a BM and can manage ads on their own BM
11. Use new domain, Pixel, and Pages that were not previously used on the restricted account or BM

---

## Disabled: Business Manager

1. Find out what caused the restriction
2. Reach out to support via chat and email
   - State all the preventions you are making
   - Provide: ID, business product explanation, ad explanation, business verification, anything they request
   - **If re-approved:** Make sure you prevent the same thing from happening again
   - **If not approved:** Follow the steps below
3. Remove yourself from the BM and transfer it to a "backfire FB account" (a safe profile you hold in reserve)
4. Transfer ads to an existing and warmed Business Manager
   - Use different domains, ad images, links, and Dataset/Pixel
   - Check for metadata as well (image metadata can carry history)
   - Use a BM that does not share admins with the disapproved one
5. Buy a verified and reinstated BM with $250 limit or no limit
   - Add a personal FB profile that is not associated with the disabled BM
   - Warm up the BM: run an engagement ad for 3+ days at small budget, get multiple ads approved
6. Set up an agency account as backup infrastructure
   - Provider options: Aurora Global, Orange Trail

---

## Disabled: Ad Account

Troubleshoot resources:
- https://www.facebook.com/business/help/968402779873601
- https://www.facebook.com/business/help/422289316306981?id=434838534925385

1. Review the reason for disapproval
   - Check in Account Quality
   - Find the real reason (message support, check BM or FB profile notifications)
2. Connect with Support
   - State all the preventions you are making
   - Provide: ID, business product explanation, ad explanation, business verification, anything they request
3. Remove all assets tied to the account and delete everything that may have caused the disapproval
4. Clear cache and cookies
5. Appeal for review through email/support with the full reason why you follow the policies
   - **If re-approved:** Done
   - **If permanently rejected:** Remove your personal profile from the ad account, then remove Pixel, Pages, debit cards, and all connected assets
6. Re-publish ads on 2 separate Business Managers
   - **If disabled due to ad content:** Use different ads, domains, pages, pixels, debit cards
   - **If disabled due to payment/location/suspicious activity:** No need to change assets — just change the debit card and publish from a static IP

---

## Restriction: Facebook Page

1. Request manual review via email/chat with Meta support
   - Provide evidence of how you follow their rules
   - Documentation: ID, payment card, business verification, authenticity proof, explanation of page use
2. Transfer the page to the backfire FB profile and remove it from the active BM
3. Build new pages in parallel (create one per week):
   - Publish content
   - Run a likes campaign
   - Get 10-30 lightweight ads approved on each page
4. Buy aged pages from Facebook community groups or resellers
   - Publish new and relevant content on them immediately

---

## Rejected: Ad

1. Check what caused the rejection
   - If the same creative was previously approved: request a manual review, or message support to clarify
2. Request manual review OR delete the ad and submit a new one
3. Publish 10-20 engagement ads with 100% approval rate
   - This improves your approved:disapproved ratio, which signals trustworthiness to the system
   - A healthy approval ratio makes future approvals faster and reduces scrutiny on borderline creatives

---

## General Recovery Principles

- **Never appeal a rejected ad without context.** If it was previously approved, appeal. If it is a new borderline creative, duplicate with small changes instead.
- **Backfire account:** Always maintain a clean, inactive "emergency" FB profile that is not connected to any active BM or ad account. Used only for BM transfers during crises.
- **Debit cards:** Keep multiple payment methods on file across different BMs. A payment issue should never be the reason campaigns go dark.
- **The faster you have backups, the faster recovery is.** Every asset you build now (warm BMs, aged pages, backup profiles) is insurance for the day something breaks.
