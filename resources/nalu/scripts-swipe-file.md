# Nalu Short-Form Scripts Swipe File

Proven short-form video script examples approved across Nalu clients. For Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts. Reference these for structure, hook patterns, open loops, and pacing.

---

## How Scripts Are Produced From a Transcript

When given a full transcript (podcast or solo video), produce **5-6 short-form scripts**:

1. **Script 1: The Compressed Version** - The entire video condensed into one actionable short. Rapidfire format. Hits every key point at speed.
2. **Scripts 2-6: Standalone Lessons** - Each one pulls a different insight, story, or takeaway from the transcript. Each has standalone value, someone who never watches the full video still gets something useful.

Every script must have:
- **2-3 hook options** (test different angles for the same content)
- **Open loops throughout** (keep the viewer watching, never let them feel they've "got it" until the payoff)
- **One core lesson** per script
- **CTA** at the end

## Script 1: Rapidfire / Compressed Whole Video

**Type:** Entire video condensed into one short
**Angle:** Speed, density, "save you time" value prop

```
Hook 1:
I read seven of the best books for business builders and figured out their key lessons to save you time:

Hook 2:
I've scaled two businesses to 30M, and here are 7 of the most effective books I've read:

Body:
One: Why Motivating People Doesn't Work.
Stop trying to motivate your team, remove what demotivates them instead. This means:
Ambiguity,
lack of ownership,
feeling controlled.

Two: Be Useful by Schwarzenegger.
Frame your goals around contribution and ask yourself "what makes me useful?" instead of "what do I want?"

Three: Clear Thinking by Shane Parrish.
Decision quality beats decision speed. So pause, take a breather before making any major decisions.

Four: The Geek Way.
Always hire for curiosity rather than experience alone. Geeks challenge assumptions and ask "why not?" when everyone says "that's how it's always been." That kind of mindset is invaluable when applied correctly.

Five: Big Bet Leadership.
Every initiative needs one directly responsible individual. Not a committee. One name.

Six: Scaling Up. You can't scale heroics, only systems. If your business can't run two weeks without you, you don't have a business. You have an expensive job.

And Seven: Legacy.
Build a culture that outlasts you. Create systems that produce heroes after you're gone.

Full breakdown in my latest video. Link down below.
```

**What makes it work:**
- Two hooks with different angles: "save you time" (value) vs. "I've done this" (credibility)
- Numbered list creates pacing and makes each point land as its own beat
- Each book gets one actionable takeaway, not a summary. The viewer leaves with something to do.
- "You don't have a business. You have an expensive job." is the open loop punchline that makes them want to hear more.
- CTA is simple, points to the full video.

## Script 2: Metaphor / Analogy Lesson

**Type:** Single concept from the transcript, explained through a vivid analogy
**Angle:** Makes abstract business concept instantly visual

```
Hook 1:
Here's what sports can teach you about why your business falls apart:

Hook 2:
If you're struggling to scale past 100 people, you need to stop playing basketball and start playing football. Let me explain what I mean.

Body:
Think about how basketball works.
It's fast, it's chaotic, and you're the pivotal player.
Every decision goes through you.
And you feel important that way, but you can't scale a business by playing basketball.

To scale, you need to play football.
In football, the players make their own decisions, the manager isn't on the pitch - they're on the sidelines being strategic.

Getting stuck between the two is what kills growth.

And the only way out?
Build systems that lets your team play football so you can get off the pitch.

Want to learn what those systems are?

CTA: Follow for the exact process to get yourself off the pitch. Link below.
```

**What makes it work:**
- Hook 2 is the stronger one: names the problem (scaling past 100), gives the answer as a metaphor, then opens a loop ("let me explain what I mean")
- The basketball/football contrast is visual and instantly understood. No jargon needed.
- "And you feel important that way" is a subtle challenge, makes the viewer question their own behaviour
- "And the only way out?" is a mid-script open loop that prevents drop-off
- CTA uses the metaphor language ("get off the pitch"), keeps it cohesive

## Script 3: Problem + Credibility + Offer (Event/Promo)

**Type:** Direct promo script, still leads with value and pain
**Angle:** "You've tried everything, here's why it hasn't worked"

```
Hook 1:
If you've hired smart people but your business still relies on you, I have some bad news.

Hook 2:
Most CEOs like you are the reason their business can't grow and they just have no idea.

Body (Version A - from Hook 1):
You might have tried a COO or a coach, yet you're still the one keeping everything together.
The problem is this: the business is still centered around you!
I lived this myself scaling tech businesses to £30M ARR and for the last decade I've helped founder-CEOs fix it.
On the 21st of April, just outside London, I'm running a one-day session to get your time back and your team running properly.
You'll leave with:
A clear plan to get you out of the bottleneck, for good
A team that owns outcomes without needing you in the room
An operating cadence so your business runs without you holding it together
Click learn more to reserve your spot.

Body (Version B - from Hook 2):
Every decision, every escalation, every moment of uncertainty routes back to you.
The business has grown but your role inside it hasn't.
I've spent the last decade working with founder-CEOs running teams of 50-100 people and 90% of the time, that's the problem. The business has scaled but your role...
So on the 21st of April, just outside London, I'm running a one-day session to fix exactly that.
You'll leave with:
A clear plan to get you out of the bottleneck
A team that owns outcomes without needing you in the room
An operating cadence so your business runs without you holding it together
Spaces are limited so click the link to reserve your spot.
```

**What makes it work:**
- Two completely different hook angles for the same offer: empathetic ("I have bad news") vs. provocative ("you're the reason")
- Both open loops immediately. Hook 1: what's the bad news? Hook 2: how am I the problem?
- Credentials land naturally mid-script, not front-loaded
- The checklist format (clear plan, team ownership, operating cadence) makes the offer tangible
- Version B's trailing "but your role..." creates a pause that mimics how you'd say it on camera

## Structural DNA: How Short-Form Scripts Work

### The 5-6 script batch from one transcript

| Script # | Type | What it does |
|----------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Rapidfire / Compressed | Entire video condensed. Numbered list, each point gets one line. Fastest pacing. |
| 2-3 | Single lesson deep-dive | One insight expanded with a metaphor, analogy, or story. Medium pacing. |
| 4-5 | Standalone takeaway | One specific, actionable lesson. Shortest format. Punchy. |
| 6 (optional) | Promo / CTA-heavy | If there's something to sell (event, video, link), lead with pain, end with offer. |

### Hook rules

- Every script gets 2-3 hook options. Test which angle wins.
- Hooks must create an open loop in the first sentence. The viewer needs a reason to keep watching.
- Hook patterns that work:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Problem + bad news** | "If you've hired smart people but your business still relies on you, I have some bad news." |
| **Provocative claim** | "Most CEOs like you are the reason their business can't grow." |
| **Compressed value promise** | "I read seven of the best books for business builders and figured out their key lessons to save you time." |
| **Credibility + list** | "I've scaled two businesses to 30M, and here are 7 of the most effective books I've read." |
| **Metaphor setup** | "If you're struggling to scale past 100 people, you need to stop playing basketball and start playing football." |
| **"Let me explain"** | Any hook that ends with "let me explain what I mean" or "here's why" |

### Open loop patterns

Open loops are what stop the viewer from swiping. Scatter them throughout, not just in the hook.

| Where | Pattern | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Hook | Tease bad news or a surprise | "I have some bad news." |
| Hook | Promise an explanation | "Let me explain what I mean." |
| Mid-script | Rhetorical pause | "And the only way out?" |
| Mid-script | Trailing thought | "The business has scaled but your role..." |
| Mid-script | Challenge assumption | "And you feel important that way, but..." |
| Pre-CTA | Promise the method | "Want to learn what those systems are?" |

### Pacing rules

- Written for spoken delivery. Read it out loud. If it doesn't flow when spoken, rewrite.
- One thought per line. New thought, new line.
- Numbered items (for rapidfire scripts) create natural rhythm and let the viewer track progress.
- Metaphors and analogies slow the pace down, which is good for single-lesson scripts where you want the insight to land.
- Promo scripts should accelerate toward the CTA. Pain slow, offer fast.

### What scripts do NOT do

- Don't front-load credentials. Weave them in after the hook has landed.
- Don't try to cover everything from the transcript. One lesson per script.
- Don't use staccato fragments as a pacing device.
- Don't end without a CTA. Every script points somewhere: follow, link, watch the full video.
- Don't write hooks that could apply to any video. The hook must be specific to the content.
