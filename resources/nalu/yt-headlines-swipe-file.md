## Hard-coded title rules

1. 60-75 characters max.
2. 6th grade language.
3. Must have mass appeal, even if niched down.

## Pattern library (DNA)

### 1) Transformation promises
- From pain state to desired identity.
- Uses fast, clear outcomes: growth, confidence, wealth, attraction.
- Formula: "How to X" + emotionally loaded benefit.

### 2) Identity and status triggers
- Calls out the viewer directly: Men, You, Normal People.
- Builds aspiration: dangerous, free, unforgettable.
- Uses identity shifts, not only tactics.

### 3) Curiosity with controlled specificity
- Numbers and time windows increase click pull: 7, 90 days, 36 years, 26 minutes.
- Mystery hooks: unsolved, hidden proof, dark laws.
- Balances known topic + unknown mechanism.

### 4) Contrarian and reframe mechanics
- "X is hard, until..."
- "You're doing it wrong" style without saying it directly.
- Repositions common beliefs with a simple new frame.

### 5) Credibility stacking
- "Proven", "Best", "Start Here", "Unfiltered", "New".
- Optional expert attribution with pipe style: "| Name".
- Uses authority cues while staying readable.

### 6) Parenthetical amplification
- Clarifies promise, audience, or edge case.
- Examples: (No Matter Your Size), (For Normal People), (Naturally).
- Parentheses add emotional or practical context without bloating core line.

### 7) Emotionally charged but plain wording
- Simple words, high-stakes meaning.
- Leverages fear, desire, urgency, relief.
- Avoids jargon-heavy phrasing.

### 8) Mass appeal through universal motives
- Money, health, attraction, identity, freedom, meaning.
- Even niche topics connect to universal outcomes.
- Topic-specific words are framed through broad human benefit.

## Operator note

When writing titles, optimise for this order:
1) Emotional pull
2) Clarity of outcome
3) Curiosity gap
4) Character length fit
5) Broad relevance
