## Writing Patterns & Conventions

### Hooks That Work
Hooks across the proven copy follow a small set of repeatable patterns:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Diagnostic question** | "Could your business run for two weeks without you?" |
| **Paradox** | "You've hired smart people but the business still relies on you" |
| **Mirror + label** | "Be honest — you're still running the show" |
| **Contrast** | "Revenue's up. Team's bigger. And yet you're more tired" |
| **Identity call-out** | "Founder-CEO, is this you?" / "Most CEOs like you..." |

### Recurring Proof Structures
- **Personal credibility:** "Scaled two companies to £30m ARR" / "coached 100+ founders"
- **Specificity:** Exact numbers (£100k cash-flow, 3 days back, 50–100 people, 90 days)
- **Guarantee as proof:** Money-back + keep materials removes risk and signals confidence

### Body Copy Formula (Ads)
1. Hook (question, paradox, or mirror)
2. Agitate (paint the daily reality — every decision routes back to you)
3. Credibility bridge (Dominic's experience, one sentence)
4. Offer (date, format, location — concrete details)
5. Three bullet outcomes (always three, always checkbox format)
6. Guarantee
7. CTA (reserve your spot / book a fit call / click learn more)

### Three-Bullet Outcome Set (Reusable)
The three outcomes stay consistent across almost all copy. Exact wording varies but the structure holds:

1. **Get out of the bottleneck** — clear plan, map of where you're absorbing pressure
2. **Team owns outcomes** — leadership team that decides and solves without you
3. **Operating cadence** — weekly rhythm so the business runs without you in the middle

### Dominic's Tone Preferences
Based on his rewrites vs the originals:

- **Shorter sentences.** He cuts filler and tightens phrasing.
- **Less salesy.** Removes urgency language ("spaces are limited"), replaces with earned confidence ("if it's a fit, I'll invite you in").
- **More measured claims.** "That's often a sign" instead of "that's what happens." Softens absolutes.
- **Gated CTA.** Prefers "book a fit call" over "reserve your spot" — positions the cohort as selective, not open-enrollment.
- **UK English.** Centre, programme, organised.
- **No emojis.** Checkmarks (tick marks) are fine in ad copy but no emoji in scripts or landing pages.

### Words & Phrases That Recur

- "What gets you to 50 breaks on the way to 100"
