# dm-6-beliefs

---

## 1. "I believe I am the bottleneck in my business, even if I don't like admitting it."

If they still believe the problem is primarily people, market, timing, or systems, they will keep searching for external fixes and reject advisory help.

This belief reframes the pain from *external frustration* to *internal leverage*.

---

## 2. "I believe more effort, intelligence, or hustle from me will not fix this."

Until this belief lands, the founder will default to pushing harder, working longer, or 'just getting through this phase'.

This belief breaks the addiction to self-sacrifice as a solution.

---

## 3. "I believe the real problem is structural, not motivational or tactical."

They must see that:

- Hiring failed because the structure was wrong
- Frameworks failed because the role of the founder never changed
- Advice failed because it treated symptoms

Without this belief, Monkhouse & Company gets lumped in with generic coaching.

---

## 4. "I believe most advice I've tried failed because it didn't deal with how the business depends on me."

This belief preserves their ego while reopening hope.

It reframes past failures as:

- Not personal incompetence
- Not bad luck
- But incomplete diagnosis

This is critical to overcoming coaching scepticism.

---

## 5. "I believe it's possible to scale without sacrificing my health, relationships, or sanity."

If they believe suffering is the price of success, they will tolerate pain instead of resolving it.

This belief cracks the martyr narrative and creates emotional permission to choose a different path.

---

## 6. "I believe someone who has lived this exact stage can help me redesign how the business runs without blowing it up."

This belief unlocks trust.

Not belief in:

- Coaching
- Consulting
- Frameworks

But belief in **experience-led, founder-level guidance**.

Without this, the final decision never feels safe.
